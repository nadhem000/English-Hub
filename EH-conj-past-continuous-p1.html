Past Continuous Tense: Generality

Learn to talk about actions in progress at a specific time in the past

Introduction to Past Continuous

üìö What is the Past Continuous Tense?

Definition:

The Past Continuous tense is used to talk about actions that were in progress at a specific time in the past.

When to use:
  • Actions in progress at a specific time in the past
  • Two actions happening at the same time in the past
  • Background description in a story
  • Interrupted actions in the past
Examples:
I was working at 5 PM yesterday.
She was reading when I called her.
They were playing soccer all afternoon.
He was studying when the power went out.

Basic Structure

üî§ How to Form the Past Continuous

Basic Rule:

Use the correct form of "to be" in past (was/were) + verb-ing.

Subject Conjugation Example (work)
I was working I was working yesterday.
You were working You were working last night.
He was working He was working at the office.
She was working She was working on a project.
It was working It was working properly.
We were working We were working together.
They were working They were working all day.
Negative Form:

Add "not" after the form of "to be".

I was not working yesterday.
She was not working at that time.
They were not working late.
Question Form:

Invert the subject and the form of "to be".

Were you working yesterday?
Was she working at 5 PM?
Were they working together?

The Verb "To Be" in Past Continuous

üî§ Using "To Be" in Past Continuous

Important Note:

The verb "to be" is used as a helping verb in Past Continuous. It is not used in the -ing form.

Subject Conjugation Example
I was being I was being careful yesterday.
You were being You were being kind to everyone.
He was being He was being helpful all day.
She was being She was being patient with the children.
It was being It was being difficult to understand.
We were being We were being quiet during the movie.
They were being They were being friendly to new students.
Negative Form:

Add "not" after the form of "to be".

I was not being lazy.
She was not being rude.
They were not being noisy.
Question Form:

Invert the subject and the form of "to be".

Was I being helpful?
Was she being careful?
Were they being friendly?

The Verb "To Have" in Past Continuous

üî§ Using "To Have" in Past Continuous

Important Note:

"To have" in Past Continuous usually means "experiencing" or "participating in" something in the past.

Subject Conjugation Example
I was having I was having lunch at noon.
You were having You were having a good time yesterday.
He was having He was having trouble with the computer.
She was having She was having a party last weekend.
It was having It was having an effect on everyone.
We were having We were having a meeting at 3 PM.
They were having They were having fun at the beach.
Negative Form:

Add "not" after the form of "to be".

I was not having fun.
She was not having problems.
They were not having a party.
Question Form:

Invert the subject and the form of "to be".

Were you having a good time?
Was she having trouble?
Were they having a meeting?

Common Verbs in Past Continuous

About Verbs in Past Continuous:

All verbs follow the same pattern: form of "to be" in past (was/were) + verb-ing.

Verb Continuous Form Example
go going I was going to school when I saw her.
do doing He was doing his homework at 7 PM.
have having She was having breakfast when I called.
eat eating We were eating dinner at that time.
see seeing They were seeing a movie yesterday.
take taking I was taking a bus to work.
More Examples:
I was going to the store when it started raining.
She was doing her homework after school yesterday.
We were having a great time at the party last night.
He was eating breakfast when the phone rang.
