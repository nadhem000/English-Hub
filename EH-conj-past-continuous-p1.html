I. 1. Introduction to Past Continuous

📘 What is the Past Continuous Tense?

📘 Definition:

The Past Continuous tense is used to talk about actions that were in progress at a specific time in the past. It often sets the scene or describes interrupted actions.

⏳ When to use:
  • Actions in progress at a specific time in the past
  • Two actions happening at the same time in the past
  • Background description in a story
  • Interrupted actions in the past
I was working at 5 PM yesterday.
She was reading when I called her.
They were playing soccer all afternoon.
He was studying when the power went out.

I. 2. Basic Structure & Conjugation

🔧 Form: was/were + verb-ing

📘 Rule:

Use the past form of "to be" (was/were) + the -ing form of the main verb. The structure changes for negative and question forms.

SubjectConjugationExample (work)
Iwas workingI was working yesterday.
Youwere workingYou were working last night.
He/She/Itwas workingHe was working at the office.
Wewere workingWe were working together.
Theywere workingThey were working all day.
❌ Negative Form:

Add "not" after was/were: was not (wasn't), were not (weren't).

I was not working yesterday.
She wasn't working at that time.
They were not working late.
❓ Question Form:

Invert the subject and was/were.

Were you working yesterday?
Was she working at 5 PM?
Were they working together?

💡 The Verb "To Be" in Past Continuous

The verb "to be" can be used as a main verb in Past Continuous (was/were being + adjective/noun). It describes temporary behavior or states.

SubjectConjugationExample
Iwas beingI was being careful yesterday.
Youwere beingYou were being kind to everyone.
He/She/Itwas beingHe was being helpful all day.
Wewere beingWe were being quiet during the movie.
Theywere beingThey were being friendly to new students.

⚡ The Verb "To Have" in Past Continuous

"To have" in Past Continuous usually means "experiencing" or "participating in" something in the past.

SubjectConjugationExample
Iwas havingI was having lunch at noon.
Youwere havingYou were having a good time yesterday.
He/She/Itwas havingHe was having trouble with the computer.
Wewere havingWe were having a meeting at 3 PM.
Theywere havingThey were having fun at the beach.

📚 Common Verbs in Past Continuous

All verbs follow the same pattern: was/were + -ing.

I was going to the store when it started raining.
She was doing her homework after school yesterday.
We were having a great time at the party last night.
He was eating breakfast when the phone rang.

I. 3. Common Uses of Past Continuous

💬 Specific past situations & narrative context

🕒 Actions in progress at a specific time:

Use Past Continuous to emphasize that an action was happening at a precise past moment.

At 8 PM last night, I was watching a movie.
This time yesterday, we were flying to Paris.
⚡ Interrupted actions:

A longer action (Past Continuous) is interrupted by a shorter action (Past Simple).

I was sleeping when the phone rang.
They were playing football when it started to rain.
🔄 Simultaneous past actions:

Two or more actions happening at the same time in the past.

While I was cooking, my sister was listening to music.
The children were playing outside while their parents were gardening.
📖 Background description in stories:

Set the scene in a narrative.

The sun was shining, birds were singing, and I was walking through the park.
