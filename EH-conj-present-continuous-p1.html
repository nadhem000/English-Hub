Present Continuous Tense: Generality

Learn to talk about actions happening now and temporary situations

Introduction to Present Continuous

üìö What is the Present Continuous Tense?

Definition:

The Present Continuous tense is used to talk about actions that are happening right now, at this moment.

When to use:
  • Actions happening right now
  • Temporary situations
  • Changing situations
  • Future arrangements
Examples:
I am working right now.
She is reading a book.
They are playing soccer.
He is studying for his exam.

Basic Structure

üî§ How to Form the Present Continuous

Basic Rule:

Use the correct form of "to be" (am, is, are) + verb-ing.

Subject Conjugation Example (work)
I am working I am working now.
You are working You are working here.
He is working He is working at a bank.
She is working She is working hard.
It is working It is working well.
We are working We are working together.
They are working They are working late.
Negative Form:

Add "not" after the form of "to be".

I am not working now.
She is not working here.
They are not working late.
Question Form:

Invert the subject and the form of "to be".

Are you working now?
Is she working hard?
Are they working together?

The Verb "To Be" in Present Continuous

üî§ Using "To Be" in Present Continuous

Important Note:

The verb "to be" is used as a helping verb in Present Continuous. It is not used in the -ing form.

Subject Conjugation Example
I am being I am being careful.
You are being You are being kind.
He is being He is being helpful.
She is being She is being patient.
It is being It is being difficult.
We are being We are being quiet.
They are being They are being friendly.
Negative Form:

Add "not" after the form of "to be".

I am not being lazy.
She is not being rude.
They are not being noisy.
Question Form:

Invert the subject and the form of "to be".

Am I being helpful?
Is she being careful?
Are they being friendly?

The Verb "To Have" in Present Continuous

üî§ Using "To Have" in Present Continuous

Important Note:

"To have" in Present Continuous usually means "experiencing" or "participating in" something.

Subject Conjugation Example
I am having I am having lunch.
You are having You are having a good time.
He is having He is having trouble.
She is having She is having a party.
It is having It is having an effect.
We are having We are having a meeting.
They are having They are having fun.
Negative Form:

Add "not" after the form of "to be".

I am not having fun.
She is not having problems.
They are not having a party.
Question Form:

Invert the subject and the form of "to be".

Are you having a good time?
Is she having trouble?
Are they having a meeting?

Spelling Rules for -ing Forms

‚úçÔ∏è How to Add -ing to Verbs

Rule 1: Most verbs

Add -ing to the base form of the verb.

work ‚Üí working
play ‚Üí playing
read ‚Üí reading
Rule 2: Verbs ending in -e

Remove the -e and add -ing.

write ‚Üí writing
take ‚Üí taking
make ‚Üí making
Rule 3: Verbs ending in -ie

Change -ie to -y and add -ing.

lie ‚Üí lying
die ‚Üí dying
tie ‚Üí tying
Rule 4: One-syllable verbs ending in consonant-vowel-consonant

Double the final consonant and add -ing.

run ‚Üí running
swim ‚Üí swimming
stop ‚Üí stopping

Common Verbs in Present Continuous

About Verbs in Present Continuous:

All verbs follow the same pattern: form of "to be" + verb-ing.

Verb Continuous Form Example
go going I am going to school now.
do doing He is doing his homework.
have having She is having breakfast.
eat eating We are eating dinner.
see seeing They are seeing a movie.
take taking I am taking a bus to work.
More Examples:
I am going to the store now.
She is doing her homework after school.
We are having a great time at the party.
He is eating breakfast right now.
