I. 1. Introduction to the Present Continuous

📘 What is the Present Continuous Tense?

📘 Definition:

The Present Continuous tense is used to talk about actions that are happening right now, at this moment, or around the present time (temporary situations).

💡 When to use:
  • Actions happening right now: I am working right now.
  • Temporary situations: She is living in London for a month.
  • Changing / developing situations: The weather is getting warmer.
  • Future arrangements: They are meeting friends tomorrow.
I am working right now.
She is reading a book.
They are playing soccer.
He is studying for his exam.

I. 2. Basic Structure & Form

🔧 Form: am/is/are + verb-ing

📘 Basic Rule:

Use the correct form of "to be" (am, is, are) + the main verb with -ing ending.

SubjectConjugationExample (work)
Iam workingI am working now.
Youare workingYou are working here.
Heis workingHe is working at a bank.
Sheis workingShe is working hard.
Itis workingIt is working well.
Weare workingWe are working together.
Theyare workingThey are working late.
❌ Negative Form:

Add "not" after the form of "to be".

I am not working now.
She is not working here.
They are not working late.
❓ Question Form:

Invert the subject and the form of "to be".

Are you working now?
Is she working hard?
Are they working together?

I. 3. Special Verbs, Spelling Rules & Common Patterns

✍️ Verb "to be", "to have" & -ing spelling rules

📘 The verb "to be" in Present Continuous (helping verb):

The verb "to be" acts as a helping verb. It is not used in the -ing form except to describe temporary behaviour (am being, is being, are being).

SubjectConjugationExample
Iam beingI am being careful.
Youare beingYou are being kind.
He/She/Itis beingShe is being patient.
We/Theyare beingThey are being friendly.
📘 The verb "to have" in Present Continuous (experiencing / participating):

"To have" in Present Continuous usually means "experiencing" or "participating in" an activity.

SubjectConjugationExample
Iam havingI am having lunch.
Youare havingYou are having a good time.
He/She/Itis havingShe is having a party.
We/Theyare havingThey are having fun.
📝 Spelling Rules for -ing Forms:
  • Most verbs: add -ing → work → working, play → playing
  • Verbs ending in -e: remove -e, add -ing → write → writing, take → taking
  • Verbs ending in -ie: change -ie to -y, add -ing → lie → lying, die → dying
  • One-syllable CVC: double final consonant → run → running, swim → swimming, stop → stopping
📌 Common Verbs in Present Continuous:
I am going to the store now.
She is doing her homework after school.
We are having a great time at the party.
He is eating breakfast right now.
