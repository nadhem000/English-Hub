I. 1. Negative Forms in Present Perfect

🚫 Basic negative structure & examples

📘 Formula:

[Subject] + have/has + not + past participle (rest of sentence). Contractions: haven’t / hasn’t.

📘 Negative with "to be"
SubjectPositiveNegative
II have been to Paris.I have not been to Paris.
YouYou have been helpful.You have not been helpful.
HeHe has been patient.He has not been patient.
SheShe has been kind.She has not been kind.
WeWe have been quiet.We have not been quiet.
TheyThey have been friendly.They have not been friendly.

🗣️ Contractions: I haven't been, she hasn't been, etc.

📘 Negative with "to have" (as main verb)
SubjectPositiveNegative
II have had lunch.I have not had lunch.
YouYou have had a good time.You have not had a good time.
HeHe has had trouble.He has not had trouble.
SheShe has had a party.She has not had a party.
WeWe have had a meeting.We have not had a meeting.
TheyThey have had fun.They have not had fun.
📘 Negative with other verbs (incl. irregular)
SubjectPositiveNegative
II have worked here.I have not worked here.
SheShe has gone home.She has not gone home.
TheyThey have taken a break.They have not taken a break.
I haven't gone to the store. / She hasn't done her homework.

I. 2. Passive Voice in Present Perfect

🔄 Structure: have/has + been + past participle

📘 Formula:

Subject + have/has + been + past participle (+ by + agent). The focus is on the action receiver.

Active VoicePassive Voice
I have written a letter.A letter has been written by me.
She has taught the students.The students have been taught by her.
They have built houses.Houses have been built by them.
❌ Negative Passive:

Add "not" after have/has: The house has not been built. / The car hasn't been fixed.

❓ Question Form:

Invert subject and have/has: Has the house been built? / Have the students been taught?

The report has been written by the assistant.
The windows haven't been cleaned yet.

I. 3. Common Idioms in Present Perfect

💡 Idioms with present perfect for experiences

IdiomMeaningExample
turned over a new leafstarted behaving betterHe's turned over a new leaf since last year.
been through the millhad many difficult experiencesShe's been through the mill with that project.
gone the extra milemade extra effortThey've gone the extra mile to help us.
seen better daysin poor condition nowThis car has seen better days.
hit the jackpotgreat success or luckWe've hit the jackpot with this new client.
bitten the bulletdone something difficult but necessaryI've bitten the bullet and started my diet.
I've turned over a new leaf and started exercising regularly.
We've hit the jackpot with our new business idea.
