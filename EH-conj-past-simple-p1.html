I. 1. Introduction to Simple Past

📘 What is the Simple Past Tense?

📘 Definition:

The Simple Past tense is used to talk about actions or events that happened in the past and are now completed.

⏳ When to use:
  • Completed actions in the past
  • Past habits or routines
  • Past facts or general truths
  • Sequences of past actions
I worked at a bank last year.
She visited her grandmother yesterday.
They played soccer when they were children.
He lived in Paris for five years.

I. 2. The Verb "To Be" in Past

🔧 Conjugation of "To Be" in Past

SubjectConjugationExample
IwasI was a student.
YouwereYou were my friend.
He/She/ItwasHe was a teacher.
WewereWe were here.
TheywereThey were students.
❌ Negative Form:

Add "not" after was/were: was not (wasn't), were not (weren't).

I was not a doctor.
She was not tired.
They were not here.
❓ Question Form:

Invert the subject and was/were.

Was I late?
Was she a teacher?
Were they ready?

I. 3. The Verb "To Have" in Past

⚡ Conjugation of "To Have" in Past

SubjectConjugationExample
IhadI had a car.
YouhadYou had a book.
He/She/IthadHe had a cat.
WehadWe had time.
TheyhadThey had money.
❌ Negative Form:

Use "did not" (didn't) + have.

I didn't have a car.
She didn't have a dog.
They didn't have time.
❓ Question Form:

Use "did" + subject + have.

Did you have a car?
Did she have a dog?
Did they have time?

I. 4. Regular Verbs in Past

📝 Basic Rule: add -ed

📘 Rule:

For most regular verbs, add -ed to the base form for all subjects.

SubjectConjugationExample (work)
IworkedI worked yesterday.
YouworkedYou worked here.
He/She/ItworkedHe worked at a bank.
WeworkedWe worked together.
TheyworkedThey worked late.
✍️ Spelling Rules for -ed:
  • Most verbs: add -ed (work → worked)
  • Verbs ending in -e: add -d (live → lived)
  • Verbs ending in consonant + y: change y to i and add -ed (study → studied)
  • Verbs ending in vowel + y: add -ed (play → played)
  • One-syllable verbs CVC: double final consonant + -ed (stop → stopped)
❌ Negative Form:

Use "did not" (didn't) + base form of verb.

I didn't work yesterday.
She didn't work here.
❓ Question Form:

Use "did" + subject + base form.

Did you work yesterday?
Did they work together?

I. 5. Common Irregular Verbs

⚠️ Irregular past forms must be memorized

📘 About Irregular Verbs:

Some verbs don't follow the regular -ed pattern. They have their own unique past forms.
