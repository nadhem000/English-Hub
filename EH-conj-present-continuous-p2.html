Present Continuous: Questions & Adverbs

Learn to form questions and use time expressions correctly in the present continuous

Forming Questions in Present Continuous

‚ùì How to Form Questions

Basic Question Structure:

In the present continuous tense, we form questions by inverting the subject and the appropriate form of the verb "to be" (am, is, are).

Types of Questions:
  • Yes/No Questions (questions that can be answered with yes or no)
  • Wh- Questions (questions that begin with what, where, when, why, who, how)
  • Negative Questions

Questions with "To Be" in Present Continuous

üî§ Question Formation with "To Be"

Step-by-Step Process:

In the present continuous tense, "to be" is the auxiliary verb. We form questions by placing the appropriate form of "to be" before the subject.

Formula: [Am/Is/Are] + [Subject] + [Verb-ing] + [Rest of Sentence] + ?

Subject Statement Question
I I am studying English. Am I studying English?
You You are watching TV. Are you watching TV?
He He is working now. Is he working now?
She She is cooking dinner. Is she cooking dinner?
We We are playing soccer. Are we playing soccer?
They They are waiting for the bus. Are they waiting for the bus?
Wh- Questions with Present Continuous:

For Wh- questions, place the question word at the beginning, then follow with the inverted subject and verb.

What are you doing?
Where is she going?
Why are they running?

Questions with "To Have" in Present Continuous

üî§ Question Formation with "To Have"

Step-by-Step Process:

When "to have" is used as a main verb in the present continuous, we form questions by inverting the subject and the appropriate form of "to be" (am, is, are).

Formula: [Am/Is/Are] + [Subject] + [Having] + [Rest of Sentence] + ?

Subject Statement Question
I I am having a good time. Am I having a good time?
You You are having problems. Are you having problems?
He He is having a party. Is he having a party?
She She is having lunch. Is she having lunch?
We We are having a meeting. Are we having a meeting?
They They are having fun. Are they having fun?
Wh- Questions with "To Have":

For Wh- questions, place the question word at the beginning, then follow with "am/is/are," subject, "having," and the rest of the sentence.

What are you having for dinner?
Where is she having her birthday party?
Why are they having so much trouble?

Questions with Other Verbs

üìù Question Formation with Regular and Irregular Verbs

Step-by-Step Process:

For all other verbs in the present continuous, we form questions by inverting the subject and the appropriate form of "to be" (am, is, are).

Formula: [Am/Is/Are] + [Subject] + [Verb-ing] + [Rest of Sentence] + ?

Subject Statement Question
I I am reading a book. Am I reading a book?
You You are studying for the exam. Are you studying for the exam?
He He is playing football. Is he playing football?
She She is writing a letter. Is she writing a letter?
We We are watching a movie. Are we watching a movie?
They They are cooking dinner. Are they cooking dinner?
Wh- Questions with Other Verbs:

For Wh- questions, place the question word at the beginning, then follow with "am/is/are," subject, verb-ing, and the rest of the sentence.

What are you reading?
Where is he going?
Why are they laughing?
Questions with Irregular Verbs:

Even with irregular verbs, we still use the same question structure in the present continuous tense.

Are you doing your homework? (do ‚Üí doing)
Is she making dinner? (make ‚Üí making)
Are they taking a break? (take ‚Üí taking)

Time Expressions in Present Continuous

‚è±Ô∏è What Are Time Expressions?

Definition:

Time expressions tell us when something is happening. They are commonly used with the present continuous tense to indicate that an action is happening now or around now.

Common Time Expressions in Present Continuous:
Time Expression Meaning Example
now at this moment I am studying now.
right now at this exact moment She is calling you right now.
at the moment currently We are working at the moment.
currently at the present time He is currently living in Paris.
today on this day They are visiting their grandparents today.
this week during the current week I am working on a project this week.
Placement in Sentences:

1. Usually at the end of the sentence:

I am studying English now.
She is working at the moment.
They are playing football today.

2. Sometimes at the beginning for emphasis:

Right now, I am studying English.
At the moment, she is working.
Time Expressions in Questions:

In questions, time expressions usually come at the end of the question.

Are you studying now?
What is she doing at the moment?
Where are they going today?
