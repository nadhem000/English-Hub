Simple Past Tense: Generality

Learn to talk about completed actions and events in the past

Introduction to Simple Past

üìö What is the Simple Past Tense?

Definition:

The Simple Past tense is used to talk about actions or events that happened in the past and are now completed.

When to use:
  • Completed actions in the past
  • Past habits or routines
  • Past facts or general truths
  • Sequences of past actions
Examples:
I worked at a bank last year.
She visited her grandmother yesterday.
They played soccer when they were children.
He lived in Paris for five years.

The Verb "To Be" in Past

üî§ Conjugation of "To Be" in Past

Subject Conjugation Example
I was I was a student.
You were You were my friend.
He was He was a teacher.
She was She was happy.
It was It was a beautiful day.
We were We were here.
They were They were students.
Negative Form:

Add "not" after the verb.

I was not a doctor.
She was not tired.
They were not here.
Question Form:

Invert the subject and the verb.

Was I late?
Was she a teacher?
Were they ready?

The Verb "To Have" in Past

üî§ Conjugation of "To Have" in Past

Subject Conjugation Example
I had I had a car.
You had You had a book.
He had He had a cat.
She had She had a dog.
It had It had four legs.
We had We had time.
They had They had money.
Negative Form:

Use "did not" (didn't) + have.

I didn't have a car.
She didn't have a dog.
They didn't have time.
Question Form:

Use "did" + subject + have.

Did you have a car?
Did she have a dog?
Did they have time?

Regular Verbs in Past

üìù Conjugation of Regular Verbs in Past

Basic Rule:

For most regular verbs, add -ed to the base form for all subjects.

Subject Conjugation Example (work)
I worked I worked yesterday.
You worked You worked here.
He worked He worked at a bank.
She worked She worked hard.
It worked It worked well.
We worked We worked together.
They worked They worked late.
Spelling Rules for -ed:
  • Most verbs: add -ed (work ‚Üí worked)
  • Verbs ending in -e: add -d (live ‚Üí lived)
  • Verbs ending in consonant + y: change y to i and add -ed (study ‚Üí studied)
  • Verbs ending in vowel + y: add -ed (play ‚Üí played)
  • One-syllable verbs ending in consonant-vowel-consonant: double the final consonant and add -ed (stop ‚Üí stopped)
Negative Form:

Use "did not" (didn't) + base form of verb.

I didn't work yesterday.
She didn't work here.
They didn't work late.
Question Form:

Use "did" + subject + base form of verb.

Did you work yesterday?
Did she work hard?
Did they work together?

About Irregular Verbs:

Some verbs don't follow the regular -ed pattern. They have their own unique past forms that must be memorized.

Verb Past Form Example
go went I went to school yesterday.
do did He did his homework.
have had She had a cat.
eat ate We ate dinner at 7 PM.
see saw They saw a movie last night.
take took I took a bus to work.
More Examples:
I went to the gym yesterday.
She did her homework after school.
We had a great time at the party.
He ate breakfast at 8 AM.
