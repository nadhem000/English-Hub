Must & Have to: Real-World Application & Common Pitfalls

Avoid frequent mistakes, use idioms naturally, and practice in real-life contexts

Common Mistakes with Must & Have to

⚠️ Frequent errors

❌ Using 'must' for past obligation

Incorrect: He must go yesterday. ✅ Correct: He had to go yesterday. (Must has no past form; use "had to")

❌ Confusing 'mustn't' and 'don't have to'

Incorrect: You don't have to smoke here. (if you mean prohibition) ✅ Correct: You mustn't smoke here. (mustn't = prohibition; don't have to = not necessary)

❌ Incorrect question form with 'have to'

Incorrect: Have you to leave? ✅ Correct: Do you have to leave? (Use do/does for questions)

❌ Overusing 'must' in informal contexts

In casual conversation, 'must' can sound very strong or formal. You have to try this cake! is more natural than You must try this cake!

❌ Double modals with must/have to

Incorrect: He must can do it. ✅ Correct: He must be able to do it. or He can do it.

Idiomatic Expressions with Must/Have to

💬 Common fixed phrases

ExpressionMeaningExample
must-haveessential itemA good dictionary is a must-have for learners.
have to hand it to someonegive credit or praiseI have to hand it to you – you did a great job.
if you mustif you insist (often reluctant)If you must know, I'm seeing someone.
must-see / must-readhighly recommendedThis film is a must-see.
have got toinformal necessityI've got to go now.

Contextual Practice: At the Office

💼 Using modals for rules and polite obligations

Sample dialogue:

Manager: "All employees must complete the safety training by Friday."
Employee: "Do we have to do it during work hours?"
Manager: "Yes, you have to finish it by the deadline. Also, you mustn't share your login details."
Employee: "Understood. I have to say, the new software is a must-have for our team."
Manager: "I have to hand it to the IT department – they did a great job."
Employee: "If you must assign extra work, I hope it's interesting!"

Notice the use of must, have to, mustn't, and idioms in a workplace setting.
