III. 1. Negative Forms in Simple Future

🚫 Using "will not" / "won't" and "going to" negatives

📘 Negative with "will":

Add "not" after "will": will not → contraction won't. Formula: Subject + will not + base verb.

I will not (won't) study tomorrow.
She won't finish the work.
📘 Negative with "going to":

Add "not" after the appropriate form of "to be": am/is/are + not + going to + base verb.

I am not going to study tomorrow.
They aren't going to buy a car.

III. 2. Passive Voice in Simple Future

🔄 Structure: will be + past participle

📘 Forming passive voice:

Use will be followed by the past participle of the main verb. The agent (by + someone) can be included if necessary.

Active VoicePassive Voice
I will write a letter.A letter will be written by me.
She will teach the students.The students will be taught by her.
They will sell fruits.Fruits will be sold by them.
❌ Negative passive:

Add "not" after "will": will not be + past participle.

The book will not be read by students.
The car won't be fixed by him.

III. 3. Common Future Tense Idioms

💡 Expressions for everyday future situations

📘 Useful future idioms:
  • Cross that bridge when we come to it – deal with a problem when it happens
  • Hit the road – begin a journey
  • Turn over a new leaf – start behaving better
  • Bite the bullet – endure a painful experience
  • Go the extra mile – make extra effort
We'll cross that bridge when we come to it.
I'll go the extra mile to finish this project.
She'll turn over a new leaf starting next month.
