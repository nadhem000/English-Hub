III. 1. Negative Forms in Future Perfect

🚫 How to form negatives

📘 Basic Rule:

Add "not" after the auxiliary "will" (contraction: won't) + "have" + past participle for all subjects.

I will not (won't) have finished my work.
She will not have been to London.
They will not have had breakfast by then.
SubjectNegative FormExample
Iwill not / won't have workedI won't have worked there by June.
Youwill not / won't have workedYou won't have worked long enough.
Hewill not / won't have workedHe won't have worked at that company.
Shewill not / won't have beenShe won't have been to Paris.

Wewill not / won't have hadWe won't have had time.
Theywill not / won't have seenThey won't have seen the movie.

III. 2. Passive Voice in Future Perfect

🔄 Forming the passive

📘 Structure:

The passive in future perfect is formed with: will have been + past participle. The agent may be mentioned with "by".

The work will have been finished by 6 PM.
All the tickets will have been sold by noon.
Will the report have been completed by then?
SubjectActivePassive
The reportThey will have finished it.It will have been finished.
The cakeShe will have baked it.It will have been baked.
The decisionWe will have made it.It will have been made.
The keysHe will have lost them.They will have been lost.

III. 3. Idiomatic Expressions with Future Perfect

💡 Common adverbs & fixed patterns

📘 already / just / never / ever:

These adverbs usually go after "will have" but before the past participle. Examples: I will have already finished by then. She will have just arrived. He will have never seen snow.

📘 by the time / by then:

These time expressions are frequently used with future perfect to set a deadline. Example: By the time you arrive, we will have left.

📘 surely / no doubt / likely:

Used to express assumptions about completed actions in the future. Example: They will surely have heard the news by now.
