I. 1. Introduction to May & Might

📘 What are modals "may" and "might"?

📘 Definition:

"May" and "might" are modal auxiliary verbs. They do not change form (no -s in third person) and are followed by the base form of the main verb.

💡 Core ideas:
  • May – formal permission, possibility (present/future), polite requests.
  • Might – past of may (reported speech), less certain possibility, polite suggestions.
May I come in? (permission)
It may rain later. (possibility)
He said he might be late. (reported speech)
You might want to try this. (polite suggestion)

I. 2. Basic Grammar Rules

🔧 Structure & key rules

📐 Structure:

Affirmative: subject + may/might + base verb → She may come.
Negative: subject + may not / might not + base verb → They might not know.
Question: May/Might + subject + base verb? → May I leave?

⚠️ Important:

Modals never take -s, -ing, or -ed. They are always followed by the infinitive without 'to'. The negative contraction "mightn't" is rare.

I. 3. Primary Uses of "May"

🔑 Permission, possibility & polite requests

1️⃣ Permission (formal)

Use may to ask for or give permission in formal situations.

May I borrow your pen?
You may leave now.
2️⃣ Possibility (present/future)

May expresses that something is possible (often about 50% certainty).

It may rain this afternoon.
She may be at home.
3️⃣ Polite requests (with "I")

May I is a very polite way to ask for permission.

May I have a glass of water?
May I ask a question?

I. 4. Primary Uses of "Might"

💡 Past of may, weaker possibility & polite suggestions

1️⃣ Past of may (reported speech)

In indirect speech, might is used as the past form of may.

He said he might come later.
She thought it might rain.
2️⃣ Less certain possibility (30-40%)

Might suggests a weaker possibility than may.

It might snow tonight.
They might be delayed.
3️⃣ Polite suggestions

Use might to make a suggestion in a gentle, indirect way.

You might want to try the fish.
We might consider a break.

I. 5. May vs Might – Quick Comparison

UseMayMight
Permissionformal: May I come in?rare/very formal: Might I suggest?
