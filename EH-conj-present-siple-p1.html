Simple Present Tense: Generality

Master the basic verb tense for habits, facts, and general truths

Introduction to Simple Present

üìö What is the Simple Present Tense?

Definition:

The Simple Present tense is used to describe habits, unchanging situations, general truths, and fixed arrangements.

When to use:
  • Habits and routines
  • Facts and general truths
  • Fixed arrangements and schedules
  • Feelings and states
Examples:
I work at a bank.
The sun rises in the east.
The train leaves at 5 PM.
She likes coffee.

The Verb "To Be"

üî§ Conjugation of "To Be"

Subject Conjugation Example
I am I am a student.
You are You are my friend.
He is He is a teacher.
She is She is happy.
It is It is a beautiful day.
We are We are here.
They are They are students.
Negative Form:

Add "not" after the verb.

I am not a doctor.
She is not tired.
They are not here.
Question Form:

Invert the subject and the verb.

Am I late?
Is she a teacher?
Are they ready?

The Verb "To Have"

üî§ Conjugation of "To Have"

Subject Conjugation Example
I have I have a car.
You have You have a book.
He has He has a cat.
She has She has a dog.
It has It has four legs.
We have We have time.
They have They have money.
Negative Form:

Use "do not" (don't) or "does not" (doesn't) + have.

I don't have a car.
She doesn't have a dog.
They don't have time.
Question Form:

Use "do" or "does" + subject + have.

Do you have a car?
Does she have a dog?
Do they have time?

Regular Verbs

üìù Conjugation of Regular Verbs

Basic Rule:

For most verbs, use the base form for all subjects except he/she/it. For he/she/it, add -s or -es.

Subject Conjugation Example (work)
I work I work every day.
You work You work here.
He works He works at a bank.
She works She works hard.
It works It works well.
We work We work together.
They work They work late.
Spelling Rules for -s/-es:
  • Most verbs: add -s (work ‚Üí works)
  • Verbs ending in -s, -ss, -sh, -ch, -x, -o: add -es (watch ‚Üí watches)
  • Verbs ending in consonant + y: change y to i and add -es (study ‚Üí studies)
  • Verbs ending in vowel + y: add -s (play ‚Üí plays)
Negative Form:

Use "do not" (don't) or "does not" (doesn't) + base form of verb.

I don't work on weekends.
She doesn't work here.
They don't work late.
Question Form:

Use "do" or "does" + subject + base form of verb.

Do you work here?
Does she work hard?
Do they work together?

About Irregular Verbs:

Some verbs don't follow the regular -s/-es pattern for he/she/it. They have their own unique forms.

Verb I/You/We/They He/She/It Example
go go goes She goes to school.
do do does He does his homework.
have have has It has four legs.
say say says He says hello.
More Examples:
I go to the gym every day.
She goes to the market on Saturdays.
We do our homework in the evening.
He does the dishes after dinner.
