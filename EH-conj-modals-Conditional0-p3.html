Zero Conditional: Common Mistakes & Comprehensive Practice

Avoid typical errors and apply zero conditional in communication.

Common Mistakes & How to Avoid Them

⚠️ Typical errors learners make

❌ Mistake 1: Forgetting the comma after the if-clause

Incorrect: "If you heat ice it melts." → Correct: "If you heat ice, it melts." (comma needed when if-clause comes first).

❌ Mistake 2: Using "will" in the if-clause

Incorrect: "If water will reach 100°C, it boils." → Correct: "If water reaches 100°C, it boils." (Zero conditional uses present simple in both clauses).

❌ Mistake 3: Confusing zero conditional with first conditional

Zero conditional = always true (general facts). First conditional = possible future result. Compare: "If you heat ice, it melts." (zero) vs "If it rains tomorrow, I will stay home." (first).

Punctuation Rules

📝 Comma usage in zero conditional

If-clause first → use a comma

If you heat ice, it melts.

Result clause first → no comma

Ice melts if you heat it.

Zero Conditional vs First Conditional

⚖️ Always true vs possible future

TypeStructureExample
Zero ConditionalIf + present simple, present simpleIf you drop an object, it falls.
First ConditionalIf + present simple, will + infinitiveIf you drop the glass, it will break.
