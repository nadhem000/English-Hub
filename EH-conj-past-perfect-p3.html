III. 1. Negative Forms in Past Perfect

🚫 Forming negative statements

📘 Rule:

Add "not" after the auxiliary "had" (contraction: hadn't) for all subjects. The past participle remains unchanged.

SubjectNegative FormExample
Ihad not / hadn't finishedI hadn't finished my work before he arrived.
Shehad not / hadn't beenShe hadn't been to London before.
Theyhad not / hadn't hadThey hadn't had breakfast.

III. 2. Passive Voice in Past Perfect

🔄 Form: had been + past participle

Structure:

The passive in past perfect is formed with: had been + past participle. The agent may be introduced with "by".

The letter had been sent before he arrived.
All the tickets had been sold by noon.
Had the work been finished when you checked?

III. 3. Idiomatic Expressions with Past Perfect

💡 Hardly ... when, No sooner ... than, Had it not been for, Wish / If only

✨ Hardly ... when / No sooner ... than

These emphasize that one event followed immediately after another. Often with inversion.

Hardly had I arrived when it started to rain.  |  No sooner had she left than the phone rang.

🔁 Had it not been for ...

Means "if it had not been for" – expresses that something prevented a past event.

Had it not been for your help, I would have failed.

😔 Wish / If only + past perfect

Express regret about the past.

I wish I had studied harder. / If only we had known the truth.
