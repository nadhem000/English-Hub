III. 1. Negative Forms in Past Continuous

❌ Negation with was/were + not

📘 Basic negative structure:

Add "not" after was/were. Formula: [Subject] + [was/were] + not + [verb-ing]. Contractions: wasn't, weren't.

SubjectPositiveNegative (contracted)
II was studying.I wasn't studying.
YouYou were watching TV.You weren't watching TV.
He/SheHe was working.He wasn't working.
WeWe were playing.We weren't playing.
TheyThey were having dinner.They weren't having dinner.
I wasn't having a good time at the party.
She wasn't having lunch when I arrived.
They weren't doing their homework at 8 PM.

III. 2. Passive Voice in Past Continuous

📦 was/were + being + past participle

📘 Structure:

Passive voice: [subject] + [was/were] + being + [past participle] + (by + agent). Use when the doer is unknown or less important.

Active VoicePassive Voice
I was writing a letter.A letter was being written by me.
He was fixing the car.The car was being fixed by him.
She was teaching the students.The students were being taught by her.
They were building houses.Houses were being built by them.
📘 Negative passive:

Add "not" after was/were: The book was not being read. / The car wasn't being fixed.

The house was not being cleaned at that time.
The dinner wasn't being prepared when I arrived.

III. 3. Common Past Continuous Idioms

🎭 Expressions with past continuous meaning

📘 Idiomatic expressions:

Idioms often use past continuous to describe ongoing past actions with figurative meanings.

IdiomMeaningExample
raining cats and dogsraining very heavilyIt was raining cats and dogs when I left.
beating around the bushavoiding the main topicHe was beating around the bush instead of answering.
burning the candle at both endsworking too hardShe was burning the candle at both ends to finish.
pulling my legjoking with meI thought you were pulling my leg!
spilling the beansrevealing secretsHe was spilling the beans about the surprise.
I was hitting the hay when you called last night.
They were pulling my leg about the lottery win.
