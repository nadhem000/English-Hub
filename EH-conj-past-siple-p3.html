Simple Past Tense: Negative, Passive & Idioms

Learn to form negative sentences, use passive voice, and understand common idioms in the past

Negative Forms in Simple Past

‚ùå How to Form Negative Sentences

Basic Negative Structure:

In English, we use the word "not" to form negative sentences in the simple past tense. The structure depends on the type of verb used in the sentence.

Types of Negative Sentences:
  • Negative with "To Be"
  • Negative with "To Have"
  • Negative with Other Verbs

Negative with "To Be"

üî§ Negative Formation with "To Be"

Step-by-Step Process:

With the verb "to be," we simply add "not" after the verb to form negative sentences.

Formula: [Subject] + [Was/Were] + "not" + [Rest of Sentence]

Subject Positive Negative
I I was a student. I was not a student.
You You were my friend. You were not my friend.
He He was a teacher. He was not a teacher.
She She was happy. She was not happy.
We We were here. We were not here.
They They were students. They were not students.
Contractions:

In spoken English and informal writing, we often use contractions (short forms) of "was/were + not".

I wasn't a student.
You weren't my friend.
He wasn't a teacher.
She wasn't happy.
We weren't here.
They weren't students.

Negative with "To Have"

üî§ Negative Formation with "To Have"

Step-by-Step Process:

With the verb "to have," we use "did not" (didn't) to form negative sentences.

Formula: [Subject] + "did not" + "have" + [Rest of Sentence]

Subject Positive Negative
I I had a car. I did not have a car.
You You had a book. You did not have a book.
He He had a cat. He did not have a cat.
She She had a dog. She did not have a dog.
We We had time. We did not have time.
They They had money. They did not have money.
Contractions:

In spoken English and informal writing, we often use contractions (short forms).

I didn't have a car.
You didn't have a book.
He didn't have a cat.
She didn't have a dog.
We didn't have time.
They didn't have money.

Negative with Other Verbs

üìù Negative Formation with Regular and Irregular Verbs

Step-by-Step Process:

For all other verbs (not "to be" or "to have"), we use "did not" (didn't) to form negative sentences.

Formula: [Subject] + "did not" + [Base Form of Verb] + [Rest of Sentence]

Use "did not" for all subjects (I, you, he, she, it, we, they).

Subject Positive Negative
I I worked yesterday. I did not work yesterday.
You You liked the movie. You did not like the movie.
He He played soccer. He did not play soccer.
She She studied English. She did not study English.
We We lived in Paris. We did not live in Paris.
They They spoke French. They did not speak French.
Contractions:

In spoken English and informal writing, we often use contractions (short forms).

I didn't work yesterday.
You didn't like the movie.
He didn't play soccer.
She didn't study English.
We didn't live in Paris.
They didn't speak French.
Negative with Irregular Verbs:

Even with irregular verbs, we still use "did not" to form negatives, and the main verb stays in its base form.

I didn't go to the gym yesterday. (go ‚Üí went in statements)
She didn't do her homework. (do ‚Üí did in statements)
They didn't have classes yesterday. (have ‚Üí had in statements)

Passive Voice in Simple Past

üîÑ What is Passive Voice?

Definition:

In passive voice, the subject receives the action rather than performing it. We use passive voice when the doer of the action is unknown, unimportant, or obvious from context.

Structure of Passive Voice:

Formula: [Subject] + [was/were] + [Past Participle] + [by + agent (optional)]

Example: "The book was read by students."

Forming Passive Voice:

To form passive voice in simple past tense, use the appropriate form of "to be" (was/were) followed by the past participle of the main verb.

Active Voice Passive Voice
I wrote a letter. A letter was written by me.
You cleaned the room. The room was cleaned by you.
He fixed the car. The car was fixed by him.
She taught the students. The students were taught by her.
We built houses. Houses were built by us.
They sold fruits. Fruits were sold by them.
Negative Passive Voice:

To form negative passive voice, add "not" after "was/were".

The book was not read by students.
The car was not fixed by him.
The students were not taught by her.
Question Form in Passive Voice:

To form questions in passive voice, invert the subject and "was/were".

Was the book read by students?
Was the car fixed by him?
Were the students taught by her?

Common Past Tense Idioms

üí¨ What Are Idioms?

Definition:

Idioms are expressions whose meanings cannot be understood from the literal definitions of the words that make them up. They often use the simple past tense to express completed actions or past experiences.

Common Past Tense Idioms:
Idiom Meaning Example
Bite the bullet Endure a painful experience "I bit the bullet and told my boss about the mistake."
Burn the midnight oil Work late into the night "She burned the midnight oil to finish the project."
Hit the road Begin a journey "We hit the road early in the morning."
Let the cat out of the bag Reveal a secret "He let the cat out of the bag about the surprise party."
Spill the beans Reveal secret information "She spilled the beans about their relationship."
Break the ice Make people feel more comfortable "He told a joke to break the ice at the meeting."
Using Past Tense Idioms in Sentences:

Past tense idioms are typically used to describe completed actions or experiences in the past.

"I bit the bullet and went to the dentist yesterday."
"She burned the midnight oil to study for her exam."
"They hit the road as soon as the sun rose."
