Story Text

Maria arrived at the office building feeling both excited and nervous. It was her first day at her new job as a marketing assistant at Global Solutions Inc. She had moved to this country just three months ago and was still getting used to the culture and language.

As she entered the lobby, she approached the reception desk. "Good morning, I'm Maria Santos. I'm starting today in the marketing department," she said, trying to sound confident.

The receptionist smiled warmly. "Welcome, Maria! Let me call Sarah from HR to come get you. Please have a seat in the waiting area."

While waiting, Maria reviewed the employee handbook she had received last week. She wanted to make sure she understood the company policies, dress code, and work hours. The handbook mentioned something about "flex time" which allowed employees to adjust their schedules, but she wasn't completely sure how it worked.

Sarah arrived and gave Maria a tour of the office. "This is the break room where we have coffee and snacks. The marketing department is on the third floor. And here's your cubicle," Sarah explained.

Maria's supervisor, Mr. Johnson, welcomed her and introduced her to the team. "We have a team meeting every Monday at 10 AM. We use a collaborative platform called WorkSpace for all our projects and communication."

Throughout the day, Maria tried to remember all the new information - names of colleagues, office procedures, and the technology systems. She took notes diligently and asked questions when something wasn't clear.

By the end of the day, Maria felt tired but accomplished. She had survived her first day, learned a lot, and even made a few new work friends who invited her to join them for lunch the next day.
