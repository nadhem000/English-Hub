Story Text

Sarah was so excited when her teacher, Mrs. Johnson, announced the school play. It was going to be a story about a magical forest, and Sarah wanted to be a fairy. She loved the idea of wearing a sparkly costume and flying on stage!

But when the roles were given out, Sarah got the part of a tree. She felt disappointed. A tree didn't have any lines, and it just stood still. Sarah thought it was the most boring part.

During the first rehearsal, Sarah had to stand on stage with her arms stretched out like branches. She felt silly and wanted to quit. But then she saw how hard everyone else was working. The fairies were practicing their dance, and the animals were learning their lines.

Mrs. Johnson noticed Sarah's sad face. She said, 'Sarah, the tree is a very important part. The forest wouldn't be the same without trees. You set the scene for the whole play!'

Sarah thought about what Mrs. Johnson said. She decided to be the best tree she could be. She practiced standing still and even added a little sway when the wind was supposed to blow.

On the night of the play, Sarah stood on stage in her brown and green costume. She heard the audience gasp when the curtain opened. The set looked like a real forest!

After the play, Sarah's parents told her how beautiful the forest was and how her tree made it feel real. Sarah felt proud. She learned that every part in a play is important, even a tree with no lines.

The next day, Mrs. Johnson gave Sarah a certificate for 'Best Tree in the Forest'. Sarah smiled and realized that teamwork means everyone doing their best, no matter the role.
