III. 1. Negative Forms in Simple Past

🚫 Basic negative structure

📘 Negative Structure:

Use "not" after the verb to be, or use "did not" (didn't) + base form for other verbs.

📘 Negative with "To Be"

Formula: [Subject] + [was/were] + "not" + ...

SubjectPositiveNegative
II was a student.I was not (wasn't) a student.
YouYou were my friend.You were not (weren't) my friend.
HeHe was a teacher.He was not (wasn't) a teacher.
SheShe was happy.She was not (wasn't) happy.
WeWe were here.We were not (weren't) here.
TheyThey were students.They were not (weren't) students.

📘 Negative with "To Have"

Formula: [Subject] + "did not" + "have" + ...

SubjectPositiveNegative
II had a car.I did not (didn't) have a car.
YouYou had a book.You did not (didn't) have a book.
HeHe had a cat.He did not (didn't) have a cat.
SheShe had a dog.She did not (didn't) have a dog.
WeWe had time.We did not (didn't) have time.
TheyThey had money.They did not (didn't) have money.

🔧 Negative with Other Verbs

Formula: [Subject] + "did not" + [Base Form of Verb] + ... (For all verbs including irregular)

I did not work yesterday.
She didn't like the movie.
They did not go to school.

III. 2. Passive Voice in Simple Past

🔄 Structure and examples

📘 Passive Voice Formula:

[Subject] + [was/were] + [Past Participle] + [by + agent (optional)]

Active VoicePassive Voice
I wrote a letter.A letter was written by me.
You cleaned the room.The room was cleaned by you.
He fixed the car.The car was fixed by him.
She taught the students.The students were taught by her.
They sold fruits.Fruits were sold by them.

Negative passive: was/were + not + past participle (e.g., The book was not read.)

Question passive: Was/Were + subject + past participle? (e.g., Was the book read?)
