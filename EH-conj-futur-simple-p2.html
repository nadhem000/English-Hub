Simple Future Tense: Questions & Adverbs

Learn to form questions and use adverbs of time correctly in the future

Forming Questions in Simple Future

❓ How to Form Questions

Basic Question Structure:

In the simple future tense, we use the auxiliary verb "will" to form questions for all subjects. The main verb stays in its base form.

Types of Questions:
  • Yes/No Questions (questions that can be answered with yes or no)
  • Wh- Questions (questions that begin with what, where, when, why, who, how)
  • Negative Questions

Questions with "To Be" in Future

🔤 Question Formation with "To Be" in Future

Step-by-Step Process:

With the verb "to be" in the future tense, we use "will" as the auxiliary verb to form questions.

Formula: [Will] + [Subject] + [Be] + [Rest of Sentence] + ?

Subject Statement Question
I I will be a student. Will I be a student?
You You will be my friend. Will you be my friend?
He He will be a teacher. Will he be a teacher?
She She will be happy. Will she be happy?
We We will be here. Will we be here?
They They will be students. Will they be students?
Wh- Questions with "To Be":

For Wh- questions, place the question word at the beginning, then follow with "will," subject, "be," and the rest of the sentence.

Where will you be tomorrow?
What will your job be?
How will the weather be?

Questions with "To Have" in Future

🔤 Question Formation with "To Have" in Future

Step-by-Step Process:

With the verb "to have" in the future tense, we use "will" as the auxiliary verb to form questions.

Formula: [Will] + [Subject] + [Have] + [Rest of Sentence] + ?

Subject Statement Question
I I will have a car. Will I have a car?
You You will have a book. Will you have a book?
He He will have a cat. Will he have a cat?
She She will have a dog. Will she have a dog?
We We will have time. Will we have time?
They They will have money. Will they have money?
Wh- Questions with "To Have":

For Wh- questions, place the question word at the beginning, then follow with "will," subject, "have," and the rest of the sentence.

What will you have for breakfast tomorrow?
Where will she have her party?
How many children will they have?

Questions with Other Verbs

📝 Question Formation with Regular and Irregular Verbs

Step-by-Step Process:

For all other verbs (not "to be" or "to have"), we use "will" as the auxiliary verb to form questions in the future tense.

Formula: [Will] + [Subject] + [Base Form of Verb] + [Rest of Sentence] + ?

Use "will" for all subjects (I, you, he, she, it, we, they).

Subject Statement Question
I I will work tomorrow. Will I work tomorrow?
You You will like the movie. Will you like the movie?
He He will play soccer. Will he play soccer?
She She will study English. Will she study English?
We We will live in Paris. Will we live in Paris?
They They will speak French. Will they speak French?
Wh- Questions with Other Verbs:

For Wh- questions, place the question word at the beginning, then follow with "will," subject, base form of verb, and the rest of the sentence.

What will you do tomorrow?
Where will he work next year?
How often will they exercise?
Questions with Irregular Verbs:

Even with irregular verbs, we still use "will" to form questions, and the main verb stays in its base form.

Will you go to the gym tomorrow? (go → will go)
Will she do her homework? (do → will do)
Will they have classes tomorrow? (have → will have)

Adverbs of Time in Future

⏱️ What Are Adverbs of Time?

Definition:

Adverbs of time tell us when something will happen. They are commonly used with the simple future tense to specify when an action will occur.

Common Adverbs of Time in Future:
Adverb Meaning Example
tomorrow the day after today I will work tomorrow.
next week the week after this week She will visit her grandmother next week.
next month the month after this month We will move next month.
next year the year after this year They will graduate next year.
in two days two days from now He will call in two days.
in 2030 during the year 2030 I will be 30 in 2030.
Placement in Sentences:

1. Usually at the end of the sentence:

I will go to the store tomorrow.
She will call me next week.
They will arrive in two days.

2. Sometimes at the beginning for emphasis:

Tomorrow, I will go to the store.
Next year, we will visit Paris.
Adverbs of Time in Questions:

In questions, adverbs of time usually come at the end of the question.

Will you work tomorrow?
When will she arrive next week?
Where will you be next month?
