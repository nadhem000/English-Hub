II. 1. Forming Questions in Simple Future

❓ Basic question structure with "will"

📘 Basic Question Structure:

In the simple future tense, we use the auxiliary verb "will" to form questions for all subjects. The main verb stays in its base form. Types: Yes/No Questions, Wh- Questions, Negative Questions.

Will you be at home tomorrow? (Yes/No)
What will you do next weekend? (Wh-)
Won't they come to the party? (Negative)

II. 2. Questions with "To Be" in Future

👤 Formula: Will + Subject + Be + ... ?

SubjectQuestion
IWill I be a student?
YouWill you be my friend?
HeWill he be a teacher?
SheWill she be happy?
WeWill we be here?
TheyWill they be students?
📘 Wh- Questions with "To Be":

Place the question word at the beginning, then follow with "will," subject, "be," and the rest.

Where will you be tomorrow?
How will the weather be?

II. 3. Questions with "To Have" in Future

💼 Formula: Will + Subject + Have + ... ?

SubjectQuestion
IWill I have a car?
YouWill you have a book?
HeWill he have a cat?
SheWill she have a dog?
WeWill we have time?
TheyWill they have money?
What will you have for breakfast tomorrow?
How many children will they have?

II. 4. Questions with Other Verbs

🔧 Formula: Will + Subject + Base Verb + ... ?

SubjectQuestion
IWill I work tomorrow?
YouWill you like the movie?
HeWill he play soccer?
SheWill she study English?
WeWill we live in Paris?
TheyWill they speak French?
📘 Irregular Verbs:

Even with irregular verbs, use "will" + base form.

Will you go to the gym tomorrow?
Will she do her homework?

II. 5. Adverbs of Time in Future

⏰ When will it happen?

📘 Common Adverbs of Time:

tomorrow, next week, next month, next year, in two days, in 2030 – usually at the end of the sentence, but can begin for emphasis.

I will work tomorrow.
Next week, she will visit her grandmother.
Will you be here next month?
