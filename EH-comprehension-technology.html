Technology in Education

Intermediate Level Reading Comprehension

Level: B1-B2 (Intermediate)

Reading Passage

Reading Time: 4-5 minutes

Over the past twenty years, technology has changed the way we teach and learn. Classrooms today look very different from those of the past. Instead of only chalkboards and textbooks, many schools now use interactive whiteboards, tablets, and online learning platforms. These tools help students understand difficult subjects and allow teachers to create more engaging lessons.

One of the biggest advantages of technology in education is access to information. With the internet, students can find answers to almost any question in seconds. They can watch educational videos, read articles from around the world, and even take virtual tours of museums. This makes learning more exciting and helps students become independent learners.

Another important tool is the learning management system, or LMS. Platforms like Google Classroom and Moodle allow teachers to post assignments, give quizzes, and share resources online. Students can submit their work from home and receive feedback quickly. This is especially helpful for students who miss class due to illness or other reasons.

However, using technology in the classroom also has challenges. Not all students have access to a computer or reliable internet at home. This creates a "digital divide" between those who can use technology easily and those who cannot. Schools must find ways to help all students, such as providing devices or creating computer labs after school.

Another concern is distraction. When students use tablets or laptops in class, they might be tempted to play games, watch videos, or check social media instead of paying attention. Teachers need to set clear rules and monitor how technology is used. Some schools use software that blocks non‑educational websites during lessons.

Despite these challenges, many educators believe that technology is a powerful tool when used correctly. For example, adaptive learning software can adjust the difficulty of questions based on a student's answers. This gives each student a personalised learning experience. Virtual reality (VR) can take students on field trips to ancient Rome or inside the human body – all from their classroom.

In conclusion, technology is not a replacement for good teachers. Instead, it is a tool that helps teachers do their jobs better. When used wisely, technology can make education more accessible, interesting, and effective for students everywhere. The key is to find the right balance between traditional teaching methods and new digital tools.

/ˈdɪdʒɪtl ˈlɪtərəsi/

"Schools teach digital literacy so students can safely find and evaluate online information."
Online learning
/ˌɒnˈlaɪn ˈlɜːnɪŋ/
