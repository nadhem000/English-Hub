II. 1. Nuance & Comparison

⚖️ Choosing between must and have to

📘 Why nuance matters:

Although must and have to often overlap, the choice can reflect the source of obligation (internal vs external), formality, or the speaker's attitude.

Context Must Have to
Present must + V (I must go) have/has to + V (I have to go)
Past had to (must has no past) had to (He had to work)
Future will have to (no must future) will have to (We will have to wait)
Negative must not / mustn't (prohibition) don't/doesn't have to (no necessity)
Question Must I...? (rare, formal) Do/Does ... have to...? (common)
📝 Nuance note:

In questions, "Must I?" often sounds strict or impatient; "Do I have to?" is neutral. For past obligation, only had to exists.

I must finish this project – it's my passion. (internal)
I have to submit the report by 5 PM. (external)

II. 2. Politeness, Formality & Register

🎩 How tone changes with the modal

📊 Register comparison:
Register Example Context
Informal / spoken You have to try this cake! / I've got to go. everyday conversation
Neutral You have to show your ID. general rules, instructions
Formal / written All visitors must present identification. official signs, policies
Emphatic / moral We must fight injustice. speeches, strong beliefs
I have to finish this report by 5. (neutral necessity)
You must read this book – it's life‑changing! (strong advice)

II. 3. Secondary Uses & Fixed Expressions

🔍 Beyond simple obligation

📘 Deduction (present):

She must be at work now. – logical conclusion based on evidence

📘 Deduction (past):

He must have left early. – certainty about a past event

📘 Have got to (informal):

I've got to call my mom. – strong necessity, very common in speech

📘 Will have to (future):

You will have to renew your licence. – future obligation (instead of must)

The ground is wet; it must have rained last night.
I've got to finish this essay tonight.
