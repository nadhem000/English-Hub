Simple Present Tense: Questions & Adverbs

Learn to form questions and use adverbs of frequency correctly

Forming Questions in Simple Present

‚ùì How to Form Questions

Basic Question Structure:

In English, we use auxiliary verbs (helping verbs) to form questions in the simple present tense. The structure depends on the main verb in the sentence.

Types of Questions:
  • Yes/No Questions (questions that can be answered with yes or no)
  • Wh- Questions (questions that begin with what, where, when, why, who, how)
  • Negative Questions

Questions with "To Be"

üî§ Question Formation with "To Be"

Step-by-Step Process:

With the verb "to be," we simply invert the subject and the verb to form questions.

Formula: [To Be] + [Subject] + [Rest of Sentence] + ?

Subject Statement Question
I I am a student. Am I a student?
You You are my friend. Are you my friend?
He He is a teacher. Is he a teacher?
She She is happy. Is she happy?
We We are here. Are we here?
They They are students. Are they students?
Wh- Questions with "To Be":

For Wh- questions, place the question word at the beginning, then follow with the inverted subject and verb.

Where are you from?
What is your name?
How old are you?

Questions with "To Have"

üî§ Question Formation with "To Have"

Step-by-Step Process:

With the verb "to have," we use "do" or "does" as auxiliary verbs to form questions.

Formula: [Do/Does] + [Subject] + [Have] + [Rest of Sentence] + ?

Subject Statement Question
I I have a car. Do I have a car?
You You have a book. Do you have a book?
He He has a cat. Does he have a cat?
She She has a dog. Does she have a dog?
We We have time. Do we have time?
They They have money. Do they have money?
Wh- Questions with "To Have":

For Wh- questions, place the question word at the beginning, then follow with "do/does," subject, "have," and the rest of the sentence.

What do you have in your bag?
Where does she have her classes?
How many siblings do they have?

Questions with Other Verbs

üìù Question Formation with Regular and Irregular Verbs

Step-by-Step Process:

For all other verbs (not "to be" or "to have"), we use "do" or "does" as auxiliary verbs to form questions.

Formula: [Do/Does] + [Subject] + [Base Form of Verb] + [Rest of Sentence] + ?

Use "do" for I, you, we, they. Use "does" for he, she, it.

Subject Statement Question
I I work every day. Do I work every day?
You You like coffee. Do you like coffee?
He He plays soccer. Does he play soccer?
She She studies English. Does she study English?
We We live in Paris. Do we live in Paris?
They They speak French. Do they speak French?
Wh- Questions with Other Verbs:

For Wh- questions, place the question word at the beginning, then follow with "do/does," subject, base form of verb, and the rest of the sentence.

What do you do on weekends?
Where does he work?
How often do they exercise?
Questions with Irregular Verbs:

Even with irregular verbs, we still use "do" or "does" to form questions.

Do you go to the gym? (go ‚Üí goes for he/she/it)
Does she do her homework? (do ‚Üí does for he/she/it)
Do they have classes today? (have ‚Üí has for he/she/it)

Adverbs of Frequency

‚è±Ô∏è What Are Adverbs of Frequency?

Definition:

Adverbs of frequency tell us how often something happens. They are commonly used with the simple present tense to describe routines and habits.

Common Adverbs of Frequency:
Adverb Meaning Percentage
always 100% of the time 100%
usually almost always 90%
often many times 70%
sometimes occasionally 50%
rarely not often 20%
never 0% of the time 0%
Placement in Sentences:

1. Before the main verb (except with "to be"):

I always drink coffee in the morning.
She sometimes goes to the gym.
They rarely eat fast food.

2. After the verb "to be":

He is always late.
We are usually happy.
She is never angry.

3. At the beginning or end of sentences for emphasis:

Sometimes, I read before bed.
I go to the movies occasionally.
Adverbs of Frequency in Questions:

In questions, adverbs of frequency usually come after the subject.

Do you always eat breakfast?
How often does she exercise?
Are they usually at home on weekends?
