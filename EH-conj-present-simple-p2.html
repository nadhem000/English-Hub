II. 1. Forming Questions in Simple Present

❓ Basic Question Structure & Types

📘 Question Formation Overview

In the simple present, we use auxiliary verbs (do/does) or invert the verb "to be" to form questions. Two main types: Yes/No questions and Wh- questions.

  • Yes/No questions: Do you like coffee? / Is she a teacher?
  • Wh- questions: Where do you live? / What is your name?
  • Negative questions: Don't you like tea? / Isn't she happy?

II. 2. Questions with "To Be", "To Have" & Other Verbs

📘 Questions with the verb "to be"

Invert the subject and the verb "to be". Formula: [To Be] + [Subject] + ... ?

SubjectStatementQuestion
II am a student.Am I a student?
YouYou are my friend.Are you my friend?
HeHe is a teacher.Is he a teacher?
SheShe is happy.Is she happy?
WeWe are here.Are we here?
TheyThey are students.Are they students?
Where are you from?
What is your name?
How old are you?

📘 Questions with the verb "to have"

Use "do" or "does" as auxiliary verbs. Formula: [Do/Does] + [Subject] + have + ... ?

SubjectStatementQuestion
II have a car.Do I have a car?
YouYou have a book.Do you have a book?
HeHe has a cat.Does he have a cat?
SheShe has a dog.Does she have a dog?
WeWe have time.Do we have time?
TheyThey have money.Do they have money?
What do you have in your bag?
Where does she have her classes?

✍️ Questions with other verbs (do/does + base form)

Use "do" (I, you, we, they) or "does" (he, she, it) + base verb. Irregular verbs keep base form.

SubjectStatementQuestion
II work every day.Do I work every day?
YouYou like coffee.Do you like coffee?
HeHe plays soccer.Does he play soccer?
SheShe studies English.Does she study English?
WeWe live in Paris.Do we live in Paris?
TheyThey speak French.Do they speak French?
What do you do on weekends?
Where does he work?
How often do they exercise?

II. 3. Adverbs of Frequency

⏱️ Placement & Usage with Simple Present

📘 Definition

Adverbs of frequency tell how often something happens. Common in simple present to describe routines.

AdverbMeaningFrequency
always100% of the time100%
usuallyalmost always90%
oftenmany times70%
sometimesoccasionally50%
rarelynot often20%
never0% of the time0%
📍 Placement Rules
  • Before main verb: I always drink coffee.
  • After verb 'to be': She is never late.
  • Beginning/end (emphasis): Sometimes I walk to work.
He always eats breakfast.
We are usually happy.
Do you often go to the cinema?
They rarely eat fast food.
