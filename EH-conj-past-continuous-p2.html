II. 1. Forming Questions in Past Continuous

❓ Yes/No & Wh- Questions

📘 Basic Question Structure:

In past continuous, invert the subject and past form of "to be" (was/were). Formula: [Was/Were] + [Subject] + [Verb-ing] + ?

SubjectYes/No Question Example
IWas I studying at 8 PM?
YouWere you watching TV?
He/She/ItWas he working yesterday?
WeWere we playing soccer?
TheyWere they waiting for the bus?
📘 Wh- Questions:

Add a question word (what, where, why, when, who, how) before was/were. Example: What were you doing at 8 PM? / Where was she going?

II. 2. Questions with Different Verbs

🔧 To Be, To Have and action verbs

📘 Using 'to have' as main verb:

Formula: Was/Were + subject + having + ...? Example: Were you having lunch when I called? Was she having a good time?

Was I having problems with my computer?
Were they having a meeting at 3 PM?
What were you having for dinner yesterday?
📘 Other verbs (regular/irregular):

Was/Were + subject + verb-ing. Even irregular verbs use -ing form. Example: Were you doing your homework? Was she making dinner?

Was I reading a book yesterday evening?
Why were they laughing so much?
Where was he going when you saw him?

II. 3. Time Expressions in Past Continuous

⏰ When, while, yesterday, last night, at that time

📘 Common time markers:

Time expressions tell when an action was in progress. They usually go at the end of the sentence or at the beginning for emphasis.

ExpressionMeaningExample
yesterdayon the day before todayI was studying yesterday.
last nightduring previous eveningShe was watching TV last night.
at that timeduring that specific periodWe were working at that time.
whileduring the time thatThey were eating while we talked.
Were you studying yesterday?
What was she doing last night?
Yesterday, I was working all day.
