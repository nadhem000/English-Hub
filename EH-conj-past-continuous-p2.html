Learn to form questions and use time expressions correctly in the past continuous

Forming Questions in Past Continuous

‚ùì How to Form Questions

Basic Question Structure:

In the past continuous tense, we form questions by inverting the subject and the appropriate form of the verb "to be" in past (was/were).

Types of Questions:
  • Yes/No Questions (questions that can be answered with yes or no)
  • Wh- Questions (questions that begin with what, where, when, why, who, how)
  • Negative Questions

Questions with "To Be" in Past Continuous

üî§ Question Formation with "To Be"

Step-by-Step Process:

In the past continuous tense, "to be" is the auxiliary verb. We form questions by placing the appropriate form of "to be" (was/were) before the subject.

Formula: [Was/Were] + [Subject] + [Verb-ing] + [Rest of Sentence] + ?

Subject Statement Question
I I was studying English at 8 PM yesterday. Was I studying English at 8 PM yesterday?
You You were watching TV when I called. Were you watching TV when I called?
He He was working at that time. Was he working at that time?
She She was cooking dinner at 7 PM. Was she cooking dinner at 7 PM?
We We were playing soccer yesterday afternoon. Were we playing soccer yesterday afternoon?
They They were waiting for the bus when it started raining. Were they waiting for the bus when it started raining?
Wh- Questions with Past Continuous:

For Wh- questions, place the question word at the beginning, then follow with the inverted subject and verb.

What were you doing at 8 PM yesterday?
Where was she going when you saw her?
Why were they running in the park?

Questions with "To Have" in Past Continuous

üî§ Question Formation with "To Have"

Step-by-Step Process:

When "to have" is used as a main verb in the past continuous, we form questions by inverting the subject and the appropriate form of "to be" in past (was/were).

Formula: [Was/Were] + [Subject] + [Having] + [Rest of Sentence] + ?

Subject Statement Question
I I was having a good time at the party. Was I having a good time at the party?
You You were having problems with your computer. Were you having problems with your computer?
He He was having a party last weekend. Was he having a party last weekend?
She She was having lunch when I arrived. Was she having lunch when I arrived?
We We were having a meeting at 3 PM. Were we having a meeting at 3 PM?
They They were having fun at the beach yesterday. Were they having fun at the beach yesterday?
Wh- Questions with "To Have":

For Wh- questions, place the question word at the beginning, then follow with "was/were," subject, "having," and the rest of the sentence.

What were you having for dinner yesterday?
Where was she having her birthday party?
Why were they having so much trouble with the project?

Questions with Other Verbs

üìù Question Formation with Regular and Irregular Verbs

Step-by-Step Process:

For all other verbs in the past continuous, we form questions by inverting the subject and the appropriate form of "to be" in past (was/were).

Formula: [Was/Were] + [Subject] + [Verb-ing] + [Rest of Sentence] + ?

Subject Statement Question
I I was reading a book yesterday evening. Was I reading a book yesterday evening?
You You were studying for the exam last night. Were you studying for the exam last night?
He He was playing football when it started raining. Was he playing football when it started raining?
She She was writing a letter at 5 PM. Was she writing a letter at 5 PM?
We We were watching a movie yesterday. Were we watching a movie yesterday?
They They were cooking dinner when the guests arrived. Were they cooking dinner when the guests arrived?
Wh- Questions with Other Verbs:

For Wh- questions, place the question word at the beginning, then follow with "was/were," subject, verb-ing, and the rest of the sentence.

What were you reading yesterday?
Where was he going when you saw him?
Why were they laughing so much?
Questions with Irregular Verbs:

Even with irregular verbs, we still use the same question structure in the past continuous tense.

Were you doing your homework at 7 PM? (do ‚Üí doing)
Was she making dinner when you called? (make ‚Üí making)
Were they taking a break at that time? (take ‚Üí taking)

Time Expressions in Past Continuous

‚è±Ô∏è What Are Time Expressions?

Definition:

Time expressions tell us when something was happening in the past. They are commonly used with the past continuous tense to indicate that an action was in progress at a specific time in the past.

Common Time Expressions in Past Continuous:
Time Expression Meaning Example
yesterday on the day before today I was studying yesterday.
last night during the previous evening/night She was watching TV last night.
at that time during that specific period We were working at that time.
when at the moment that He was sleeping when I called.
while during the time that They were eating while we were talking.
all day yesterday throughout the entire day before today I was working all day yesterday.
Placement in Sentences:

1. Usually at the end of the sentence:

I was studying English yesterday.
She was working at that time.
They were playing football last night.

2. Sometimes at the beginning for emphasis:

Yesterday, I was studying English.
At that time, she was working.
Time Expressions in Questions:

In questions, time expressions usually come at the end of the question.

Were you studying yesterday?
What was she doing last night?
Where were they going at that time?
