I. 1. Introduction to Should & Ought To

📘 What are modals "should" and "ought to"?

📘 Definition:

"Should" and "ought to" are modal verbs used to give advice, express weak obligation, or talk about expectations. "Should" is the most common; "ought to" is slightly more formal and often implies moral duty.

💡 Core ideas:
  • Should – advice, recommendation, expectation, weak obligation.
  • Ought to – moral obligation, advice (more formal), expectation.
You should see a doctor. (advice)
We ought to help the poor. (moral obligation)
The train should arrive soon. (expectation)
You ought to try this dish. (recommendation)

I. 2. Basic Grammar Rules

🔧 Structure & key rules

📐 Structure:

Affirmative: subject + should / ought to + base verb → She should study. / They ought to wait.
Negative: subject + should not/shouldn't / ought not to/oughtn't to + base verb → He shouldn't smoke. / You ought not to worry.
Question: Should + subject + base verb? (Ought + subject + to + base verb? is rare) → Should I go?

⚠️ Important:

Modals never take -s, -ing, or -ed. "Ought to" is always followed by "to" + infinitive. Negative contractions: shouldn't, oughtn't to (rare).

I. 3. Primary Uses of "Should"

💬 Advice, expectation & weak obligation

1️⃣ Advice / Recommendation

We use should to give or ask for advice or recommendations.

You should eat more vegetables.
Should I call her?
2️⃣ Expectation / Probability

Should can express that something is likely to happen or is expected.

The package should arrive tomorrow.
He should be home by now.
3️⃣ Weak obligation / Correctness

Should expresses what is right or a good idea, but not as strong as must.

We should respect our parents.
I should go now.

I. 4. Primary Uses of "Ought To"

⚖️ Moral duty, formal advice & expectation

1️⃣ Moral obligation / Duty

Ought to often implies a moral duty or what is ethically right.

We ought to protect the environment.
They ought to tell the truth.
2️⃣ Advice (more formal)

Ought to can be used for advice, similar to should but slightly more formal.

You ought to see that film; it's excellent.
She ought to apologize.
3️⃣ Expectation (less common)

Like should, ought to can express that something is likely.

The train ought to be here soon.
This ought to be enough.
