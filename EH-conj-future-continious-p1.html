Future Continuous Tense: Generality

Learn to talk about actions that will be in progress at a specific time in the future

Introduction to Future Continuous

üìö What is the Future Continuous Tense?

Definition:

The Future Continuous tense is used to talk about actions that will be in progress at a specific time in the future.

When to use:
  • Actions that will be in progress at a specific time in the future
  • Predicting or guessing about future events
  • Politely asking about someone's plans
  • Talking about multiple actions happening at the same time in the future
Examples:
I will be working at 5 PM tomorrow.
She will be studying when you arrive.
They will be traveling all next week.
He will be sleeping at midnight.

Basic Structure

üî§ How to Form the Future Continuous

Basic Rule:

Use "will be" + verb-ing for all subjects.

Subject Conjugation Example (work)
I will be working I will be working tomorrow.
You will be working You will be working next week.
He will be working He will be working at the office.
She will be working She will be working on a project.
It will be working It will be working properly.
We will be working We will be working together.
They will be working They will be working all day.
Negative Form:

Add "not" between "will" and "be".

I will not be working tomorrow.
She will not be working at that time.
They will not be working late.
Question Form:

Invert the subject and "will".

Will you be working tomorrow?
Will she be working at 5 PM?
Will they be working together?

The Verb "To Be" in Future Continuous

üî§ Using "To Be" in Future Continuous

Important Note:

The verb "to be" is used as a helping verb in Future Continuous. It is not used in the -ing form.

Subject Conjugation Example
I will be being I will be being careful tomorrow.
You will be being You will be being kind to everyone.
He will be being He will be being helpful all day.
She will be being She will be being patient with the children.
It will be being It will be being difficult to understand.
We will be being We will be being quiet during the movie.
They will be being They will be being friendly to new students.
Negative Form:

Add "not" between "will" and "be".

I will not be being lazy.
She will not be being rude.
They will not be being noisy.
Question Form:

Invert the subject and "will".

Will I be being helpful?
Will she be being careful?
Will they be being friendly?

The Verb "To Have" in Future Continuous

üî§ Using "To Have" in Future Continuous

Important Note:

"To have" in Future Continuous usually means "experiencing" or "participating in" something in the future.

Subject Conjugation Example
I will be having I will be having lunch at noon tomorrow.
You will be having You will be having a good time next week.
He will be having He will be having trouble with the computer.
She will be having She will be having a party next weekend.
It will be having It will be having an effect on everyone.
We will be having We will be having a meeting at 3 PM tomorrow.
They will be having They will be having fun at the beach next month.
Negative Form:

Add "not" between "will" and "be".

I will not be having fun.
She will not be having problems.
They will not be having a party.
Question Form:

Invert the subject and "will".

Will you be having a good time?
Will she be having trouble?
Will they be having a meeting?

Common Verbs in Future Continuous

About Verbs in Future Continuous:

All verbs follow the same pattern: "will be" + verb-ing.

Verb Continuous Form Example
go going I will be going to school when you call.
do doing He will be doing his homework at 7 PM tomorrow.
have having She will be having breakfast when I arrive.
eat eating We will be eating dinner at that time.
see seeing They will be seeing a movie tomorrow.
take taking I will be taking a bus to work.
More Examples:
I will be going to the store when it starts raining.
She will be doing her homework after school tomorrow.
We will be having a great time at the party next week.
He will be eating breakfast when the phone rings.
