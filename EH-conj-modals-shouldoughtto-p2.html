II. 1. Comparing Should and Ought to Across Contexts

⚖️ Form, nuance, and usage across tenses

FormShouldOught to
Presentshould + V (I should go)ought to + V (I ought to go)
Pastshould have + past participle (I should have gone)ought to have + past participle (I ought to have gone)
Futureshould + V (The train should arrive soon)ought to + V (less common)
Negativeshould not / shouldn'tought not to / oughtn't to (rare)
QuestionShould I...? (common)Ought I to...? (very formal, rare)
💡 Nuance note:

In questions, "Should I?" is neutral; "Ought I to?" sounds very formal or old-fashioned. For past obligation or regret, only "should have" and "ought to have" exist (no simple past form).

II. 2. Politeness, Formality & Register

🗣️ Choosing the right level of formality

RegisterExample (advice/recommendation)Context
Informal / spokenYou should try this cake! / I should go now.everyday conversation
NeutralYou should talk to your boss.general advice
Formal / writtenCitizens ought to respect the law.official recommendations, moral duties
Emphatic / moralWe ought to protect the environment.speeches, ethical statements

II. 3. Secondary Uses & Fixed Expressions

📘 Past regret, expectation, and formal conditionals

UsageExampleExplanation
Past regret / criticismI should have studied harder.expresses that you didn't do something and now regret it
Past regret (ought to)You ought to have told me.same meaning, slightly more formal
Expectation (present)He should be at work by now.logical conclusion based on evidence
Expectation (past)The package should have arrived yesterday.expected but possibly didn't
Conditional (should)If you should see her, say hello.formal conditional (rare)
📌 Note:

"Should have" is very common for past regret. "Ought to have" is possible but less frequent.
