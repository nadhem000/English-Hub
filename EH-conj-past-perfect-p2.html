II. 1. Forming Questions in Past Perfect

❓ Basic question structure

📘 Rule:

In the past perfect tense, we form questions by inverting the subject and auxiliary verb "had". The main verb remains in the past participle form. Types: Yes/No questions, Wh- questions, and negative questions.

Question TypeStructureExample
Yes/NoHad + subject + past participle?Had you finished your work?
Wh-Wh- word + had + subject + past participle?What had she done?

II. 2. Questions with "To Be" in Past Perfect

🔍 Using had been in questions

Formula: Had + Subject + Been + ...?

Example: Had I been to London before? / Where had you been before the meeting?

SubjectStatementQuestion
II had been to London before.Had I been to London before?
SheShe had been a teacher.Had she been a teacher?
TheyThey had been married since 2010.Had they been married since 2010?

II. 3. Questions with "To Have" in Past Perfect

🛠️ Had + subject + had + ...?

Had I had breakfast? (Yes/No)
What had you had for breakfast? (Wh-)
How many interviews had they had?

II. 4. Time Expressions in Past Perfect

⏱️ Already, just, never, ever, before, by the time

Placement rules:

Adverbs like already, just, never go after "had" but before the past participle. "Before" and "by the time" introduce time clauses.

I had already finished when he arrived.
She had just left.
By the time we arrived, they had left.
