Avoid frequent mistakes, use idioms naturally, and practice in real-life contexts

Common Mistakes with Should & Ought to

⚠️ Frequent errors

❌ Adding 'to' after should

Incorrect: You should to study. ✅ Correct: You should study. (should + base verb)

❌ Using 'should of' instead of 'should have'

Incorrect: I should of called. ✅ Correct: I should have called. (past regret requires 'have')

❌ Forgetting 'to' after ought

Incorrect: She ought apologize. ✅ Correct: She ought to apologize. (ought + to + base verb)

❌ Confusing should/must for obligation strength

"Should" is advice/weak obligation; "must" is strong obligation/necessity. You must stop (law) vs You should stop (advice).

Contextual Practice: At a Doctor's Office

🩺 Using modals for advice, recommendation & obligation

Sample dialogue:

Doctor: "You look exhausted. You should get more rest."
Patient: "I know, but work is so demanding."
Doctor: "You ought to reduce your stress. Have you tried exercising?"
Patient: "I should have started months ago."
Doctor: "It's not too late. You ought to make a plan. And you shouldn't skip meals."
Patient: "You're right. I should listen to your advice."

Notice the use of should, ought to, should have for advice, regret, and recommendation.
