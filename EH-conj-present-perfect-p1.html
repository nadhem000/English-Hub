Learn to talk about past actions with present relevance

Introduction to Present Perfect

üìö What is the Present Perfect Tense?

Definition:

The Present Perfect tense connects the past with the present. It is used for actions that happened at an unspecified time in the past, or actions that started in the past and continue to the present.

When to use:
  • Actions that happened at an unspecified time in the past
  • Actions that started in the past and continue to the present
  • Life experiences
  • Recent past actions with present results
Examples:
I have finished my homework.
She has lived in London for five years.
They have visited Paris three times.
He has just arrived.

Basic Structure

üî§ How to Form the Present Perfect

Basic Rule:

Use "have/has" + past participle of the verb.

Subject Conjugation Example (work)
I have worked I have worked here for two years.
You have worked You have worked hard today.
He has worked He has worked at this company since 2020.
She has worked She has worked on this project for months.
It has worked It has worked well so far.
We have worked We have worked together before.
They have worked They have worked in this industry for a long time.
Negative Form:

Add "not" after "have/has".

I have not finished my work.
She has not visited that museum.
They have not seen the new movie.
Question Form:

Invert the subject and "have/has".

Have you finished your homework?
Has she visited Paris?
Have they seen the new movie?

The Verb "To Be" in Present Perfect

üî§ Using "To Be" in Present Perfect

Important Note:

The verb "to be" in Present Perfect uses "have/has been". It is used to talk about states or conditions that started in the past and continue to the present, or experiences in life.

Subject Conjugation Example
I have been I have been to London.
You have been You have been very helpful.
He has been He has been sick for three days.
She has been She has been a teacher since 2015.
It has been It has been raining all day.
We have been We have been friends for ten years.
They have been They have been married since last year.
Negative Form:

Add "not" after "have/has".

I have not been to Paris.
She has not been feeling well.
They have not been here before.
Question Form:

Invert the subject and "have/has".

Have you been to London?
Has she been working here long?
Have they been waiting long?

The Verb "To Have" in Present Perfect

üî§ Using "To Have" in Present Perfect

Important Note:

"To have" in Present Perfect uses "have/has had". It is used to talk about possession, experiences, or actions that started in the past and continue to the present.

Subject Conjugation Example
I have had I have had this car for five years.
You have had You have had enough time to finish.
He has had He has had three cups of coffee today.
She has had She has had this job since last year.
It has had It has had a big impact on our lives.
We have had We have had many opportunities.
They have had They have had a lot of problems recently.
Negative Form:

Add "not" after "have/has".

I have not had breakfast yet.
She has not had any problems.
They have not had time to finish.
Question Form:

Invert the subject and "have/has".

Have you had lunch yet?
Has she had any experience with this?
Have they had enough time to prepare?

About Verbs in Present Perfect:

Regular verbs form the past participle by adding -ed. Irregular verbs have special past participle forms that must be memorized.

Verb Past Participle Example
go gone I have gone to the store.
do done He has done his homework.
see seen She has seen that movie.
eat eaten We have eaten lunch.
write written They have written a letter.
take taken I have taken the bus to work.
More Examples:
I have gone to that restaurant many times.
She has done all her chores.
We have seen this show before.
He has eaten already.
