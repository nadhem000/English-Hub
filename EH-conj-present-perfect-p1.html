I. 1. Introduction to the Present Perfect

📘 What is the Present Perfect Tense?

📘 Definition:

The Present Perfect tense connects the past with the present. It is used for actions that happened at an unspecified time in the past, or actions that started in the past and continue to the present.

💡 When to use:
  • Actions that happened at an unspecified time in the past
  • Actions that started in the past and continue to the present
  • Life experiences
  • Recent past actions with present results
I have finished my homework.
She has lived in London for five years.
They have visited Paris three times.
He has just arrived.

I. 2. Basic Structure: Have/Has + Past Participle

🔧 Form: Subject + have/has + past participle

📘 Basic Rule:

Use "have" with I, you, we, they; use "has" with he, she, it. The main verb is in its past participle form.

SubjectConjugationExample (work)
Ihave workedI have worked here for two years.
Youhave workedYou have worked hard today.
Hehas workedHe has worked at this company since 2020.
Shehas workedShe has worked on this project for months.
Ithas workedIt has worked well so far.
Wehave workedWe have worked together before.
Theyhave workedThey have worked in this industry for a long time.
❌ Negative Form:

Add "not" after have/has: have not (haven't), has not (hasn't).

I have not finished my work.
She has not visited that museum.
❓ Question Form:

Invert the subject and have/has.

Have you finished your homework?
Has she visited Paris?

I. 3. The Verb "To Be" in Present Perfect

✨ Have/Has Been: States, experiences & ongoing conditions

📘 Important Note:

The verb "to be" in Present Perfect uses "have/has been". It refers to states, life experiences, or conditions that started in the past and continue to the present.

SubjectConjugationExample
Ihave beenI have been to London.
Youhave beenYou have been very helpful.
Hehas beenHe has been sick for three days.
Shehas beenShe has been a teacher since 2015.
Ithas beenIt has been raining all day.
Wehave beenWe have been friends for ten years.
Theyhave beenThey have been married since last year.
I have not been to Paris.
Have you been to London?

I. 4. The Verb "To Have" in Present Perfect

📌 Have/Has Had: possession & experiences

📘 Important Note:

"To have" in Present Perfect uses "have/has had". Used for possession, experiences, or actions that started in the past and continue.

SubjectConjugationExample
Ihave hadI have had this car for five years.
Youhave hadYou have had enough time to finish.
Hehas hadHe has had three cups of coffee today.
Shehas hadShe has had this job since last year.
Ithas hadIt has had a big impact on our lives.
Wehave hadWe have had many opportunities.
Theyhave hadThey have had a lot of problems recently.
