Negative Forms in Future Continuous

‚ùå How to Form Negative Sentences

Basic Negative Structure:

In English, we use "not" after "will" to form negative sentences in the future continuous tense. The structure is the same for all verbs.

Formula: [Subject] + "will not" + "be" + [Verb-ing] + [Rest of Sentence]

Types of Negative Sentences:
  • Negative with "To Be"
  • Negative with "To Have"
  • Negative with Other Verbs

Negative with "To Be"

üî§ Negative Formation with "To Be"

Step-by-Step Process:

With the verb "to be" in future continuous, we add "not" after "will" to form negative sentences.

Formula: [Subject] + "will not" + "be" + "being" + [Rest of Sentence]

Subject Positive Negative
I I will be being careful tomorrow. I will not be being careful tomorrow.
You You will be being helpful at that time. You will not be being helpful at that time.
He He will be being patient during the meeting. He will not be being patient during the meeting.
She She will be being kind to everyone. She will not be being kind to everyone.
We We will be being quiet during the ceremony. We will not be being quiet during the ceremony.
They They will be being friendly to the new students. They will not be being friendly to the new students.
Contractions:

In spoken English and informal writing, we often use contractions (short forms).

I won't be being careful tomorrow.
You won't be being helpful at that time.
He won't be being patient during the meeting.
She won't be being kind to everyone.
We won't be being quiet during the ceremony.
They won't be being friendly to the new students.

Passive Voice in Future Continuous

üîÑ What is Passive Voice?

Definition:

In passive voice, the subject receives the action rather than performing it. We use passive voice when the doer of the action is unknown, unimportant, or obvious from context.

Structure of Passive Voice in Future Continuous:

Formula: [Subject] + "will be being" + [Past Participle] + [by + agent (optional)]

Example: "The house will be being built by workers at that time."

Forming Passive Voice:

To form passive voice in future continuous tense, use "will be being" followed by the past participle of the main verb.

Active Voice Passive Voice
I will be writing a letter at that time. A letter will be being written by me at that time.
You will be cleaning the room tomorrow. The room will be being cleaned by you tomorrow.
He will be fixing the car this afternoon. The car will be being fixed by him this afternoon.
She will be teaching the students at 10 AM. The students will be being taught by her at 10 AM.
We will be building houses next month. Houses will be being built by us next month.
They will be selling fruits at the market. Fruits will be being sold by them at the market.
Negative Passive Voice:

To form negative passive voice, add "not" after "will".

The house will not be being built by workers at that time.
The car will not be being fixed by him this afternoon.
The students will not be being taught by her at 10 AM.
Question Form in Passive Voice:

To form questions in passive voice, invert the subject and "will".

Will the house be being built by workers at that time?
Will the car be being fixed by him this afternoon?
Will the students be being taught by her at 10 AM?

Common Future Continuous Idioms

üí¨ What Are Idioms?

Definition:

Idioms are expressions whose meanings cannot be understood from the literal definitions of the words that make them up. They often use the future continuous tense to describe ongoing actions in the future.

Common Future Continuous Idioms:
Idiom Meaning Example
Waiting with bated breath Waiting anxiously and excitedly "I'll be waiting with bated breath for your response."
Holding down the fort Taking care of things while someone is away "While you're on vacation, I'll be holding down the fort."
Keeping an eye on Watching or monitoring something "I'll be keeping an eye on your house while you're away."
Going the extra mile Making extra effort "Tomorrow, I'll be going the extra mile to finish this project."
Burning the midnight oil Working late into the night "This weekend, I'll be burning the midnight oil to meet the deadline."
Thinking on my feet Making quick decisions in difficult situations "During the presentation, I'll be thinking on my feet to answer questions."
Using Future Continuous Idioms in Sentences:

Future continuous idioms are typically used to describe ongoing actions or states in the future.

"I'll be waiting with bated breath for the results."
"She'll be holding down the fort while the manager is away."
"We'll be keeping an eye on the situation throughout the night."
