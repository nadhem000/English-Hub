II. 1. Asking Questions in Future Continuous

❓ Forming yes/no and wh- questions

📘 Definition:

We use Future Continuous questions to ask about actions that will be in progress at a specific future time. They are useful for polite inquiries about plans or predictions.

Will you be working at 5 PM tomorrow?
What will she be doing when I arrive?
Will they be traveling next week?
Where will you be staying during your vacation?

II. 2. Question Structure

📐 Forming questions: will + subject + be + verb‑ing

SubjectQuestion FormExample (work)
IWill I be working?Will I be working tomorrow at 5 PM?
YouWill you be working?Will you be working next week?
HeWill he be working?Will he be working at the office?
SheWill she be working?Will she be working on a project?
WeWill we be working?Will we be working together?
TheyWill they be working?Will they be working all day?
❌ Negative questions:

Add "not" after the subject or use contraction "won't". Example: Won't you be working tomorrow? / Will she not be working at that time?

❓ WH‑questions:

Place question word before "will". Example: What will you be doing? Where will she be working?

II. 3. Using Adverbs with Future Continuous

⏱️ Common adverbs and time expressions

📘 Adverb placement:

Most adverbs come after "will be" but before the main verb. Example: She will be carefully reading the instructions.

I will be studying at 5 PM tomorrow.
She will be working all day tomorrow.
Will you still be working when I arrive?
He will be carefully working on the project.

II. 4. Questions with 'To Be'

🔨 Using 'will be being'

When "to be" is the main verb, we use "will be being". Example: Will I be being careful enough?

Will you be being kind to everyone?
Won't they be being friendly?
Why will she be being careful?

II. 5. Questions with 'To Have'

🍽️ Expressing experiences or events

"To have" in Future Continuous often means experiencing or participating in something. Structure: will + subject + be having?

Will you be having lunch at noon tomorrow?
What will she be having for dinner?
Won't they be having a party next weekend?

II. 6. Questions with Irregular Verbs

🔄 Same pattern for all verbs

Will you be going to school when I call?
What will she be doing after school tomorrow?
Where will they be having the party next week?
