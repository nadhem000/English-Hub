Understanding Weather

Elementary Level Reading Comprehension

Level: A1-A2 (Elementary)

Reading Passage

Reading Time: 3-4 minutes

Emma was a curious seven-year-old girl who loved looking out the window every morning. She wanted to know what the weather would be like each day. Would it be sunny, rainy, cloudy, or windy? Emma's dad taught her how to understand different types of weather and what clothes to wear for each one.

One Monday morning, Emma looked outside and saw dark grey clouds covering the sky. The trees were moving back and forth, and she could hear the wind howling. "It's going to rain today," Emma told her mom. "I need my raincoat and umbrella." Her mom smiled and said, "You're right, Emma. The wind and dark clouds mean a storm is coming."

Emma put on her yellow raincoat and took her blue umbrella. On the way to school, big raindrops started falling. She opened her umbrella just in time. Puddles formed on the ground, and Emma jumped over them carefully. Some children ran to find shelter under a big tree, but Emma remembered her dad's advice: never stand under a tree during a storm.

At school, Emma's teacher, Ms. Green, talked about the four main types of weather: sunny, rainy, cloudy, and snowy. She showed the class pictures of each type. "When it's sunny, we wear sunglasses and hats," Ms. Green explained. "When it's rainy, we need raincoats and boots. When it's cloudy, it might rain later. And when it's snowy, we wear warm jackets and gloves."

The next day, the sun was shining brightly. There were no clouds in the sky. Emma wore her favorite pink hat and sunglasses to school. At lunchtime, she played outside with her friends. They ran and laughed in the warm sunshine. Emma felt happy because she loved sunny days the most.

On Wednesday, Emma woke up to a strange sight. Everything outside was white! Snow had fallen during the night. Emma had never seen snow before because she lived in a warm place. She was very excited. Her dad helped her put on a warm jacket, boots, and gloves. Emma made a small snowball and threw it gently at her dad. They both laughed and built a tiny snowman together.

Emma learned that every type of weather was special in its own way. She liked sunny days for playing outside, rainy days for jumping in puddles, and snowy days for making snowmen. Emma's dad was proud of her for understanding the weather so well. Now Emma checks the weather forecast every morning and helps her family prepare for the day ahead.

Vocabulary Building

Weather
/ˈweðər/
The condition of the air outside, such as sun, rain, wind, or snow.
"Emma checks the weather every morning to know what to wear."
Forecast
/ˈfɔːrkæst/
A prediction or report about what the weather will be like.
"The weather forecast said it would rain, so Emma took her umbrella."
Puddle
/ˈpʌdl/
A small pool of water, usually from rain, on the ground.
"Emma jumped over the puddles on her way to school."
Storm
/stɔːrm/
Very bad weather with strong wind, rain, thunder, and lightning.
"The dark clouds meant a storm was coming."
Snowman
/ˈsnoʊmæn/
A figure of a person made of snow, usually built by children.
"Emma and her dad built a small snowman in the yard."
