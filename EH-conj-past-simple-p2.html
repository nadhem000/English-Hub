II. 1. Forming Questions in Simple Past

❓ Basic Question Structure

📘 Basic Question Structure:

In the simple past tense, we use the auxiliary verb "did" to form questions for all subjects (except "to be"). The main verb stays in its base form. Formula: [Did] + [Subject] + [Base Verb] + ?

📌 Types of Questions:
  • Yes/No Questions (answers with yes or no)
  • Wh- Questions (what, where, when, why, who, how)
  • Negative Questions

II. 2. Questions with "To Be" in Past

📘 Using was/were

📘 Formula:

With the verb "to be", invert the subject and was/were. No "did". Formula: [Was/Were] + [Subject] + ... ?

SubjectStatementQuestion
II was a student.Was I a student?
YouYou were my friend.Were you my friend?
HeHe was a teacher.Was he a teacher?
SheShe was happy.Was she happy?
WeWe were here.Were we here?
TheyThey were students.Were they students?
Where were you yesterday?
What was your first job?
How was your vacation?

II. 3. Questions with "To Have" in Past

📘 Using did + have

📘 Formula:

Use "did" as auxiliary verb, main verb "have" stays base form. Formula: [Did] + [Subject] + [have] + ... ?

SubjectStatementQuestion
II had a car.Did I have a car?
YouYou had a book.Did you have a book?
HeHe had a cat.Did he have a cat?
SheShe had a dog.Did she have a dog?
WeWe had time.Did we have time?
TheyThey had money.Did they have money?
What did you have for breakfast?
Where did she have her party?
How many siblings did they have?

II. 4. Questions with Other Verbs

🔧 Regular & irregular verbs

📘 Formula:

[Did] + [Subject] + [Base Form of Verb] + ... ? (Use "did" for all subjects, main verb in base form even for irregular verbs)

SubjectStatementQuestion
II worked yesterday.Did I work yesterday?
YouYou liked the movie.Did you like the movie?
HeHe played soccer.Did he play soccer?
SheShe studied English.Did she study English?
WeWe lived in Paris.Did we live in Paris?
TheyThey spoke French.Did they speak French?
What did you do yesterday?
Where did he work last year?
How often did they exercise?

II. 5. Adverbs of Time in Past

⏰ yesterday, last week, ago, in 2010

📘 Common adverbs of time:
AdverbMeaningExample
yesterdaythe day before todayI worked yesterday.
last weekthe week before this weekShe visited her grandmother last week.
last monththe month before this monthWe moved last month.
last yearthe year before this yearThey graduated last year.
two days agotwo days before todayHe called two days ago.
in 2010during the year 2010I was born in 2010.

Placement: usually at the end of the sentence, or at the beginning for emphasis.

I went to the store yesterday.
Yesterday, I went to the store.
Did you work yesterday?
