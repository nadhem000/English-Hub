I. 1. Introduction to Simple Present

📘 What is the Simple Present Tense?

📘 Definition:

The Simple Present tense is used to describe habits, unchanging situations, general truths, and fixed arrangements.

💡 When to use:
  • Habits and routines: I wake up at 7 AM every day.
  • Facts and general truths: The sun rises in the east.
  • Fixed arrangements and schedules: The train leaves at 5 PM.
  • Feelings and states: She likes coffee.
I work at a bank.
The sun rises in the east.
The train leaves at 5 PM.
She likes coffee.

I. 2. The Verb "To Be"

⚡ Conjugation & Usage of "To Be"

SubjectConjugationExample
IamI am a student.
YouareYou are my friend.
HeisHe is a teacher.
SheisShe is happy.
ItisIt is a beautiful day.
WeareWe are here.
TheyareThey are students.
❌ Negative Form:

Add "not" after the verb. Examples: I am not a doctor. She is not tired. They are not here.

❓ Question Form:

Invert the subject and the verb. Examples: Am I late? Is she a teacher? Are they ready?

I. 3. The Verb "To Have"

👜 Conjugation of "To Have"

SubjectConjugationExample
IhaveI have a car.
YouhaveYou have a book.
HehasHe has a cat.
ShehasShe has a dog.
IthasIt has four legs.
WehaveWe have time.
TheyhaveThey have money.
❌ Negative Form:

Use do not (don't) or does not (doesn't) + have. Examples: I don't have a car. She doesn't have a dog. They don't have time.

❓ Question Form:

Use do or does + subject + have. Examples: Do you have a car? Does she have a dog? Do they have time?

I. 4. Regular Verbs

🔧 Conjugation Rules for Regular Verbs

SubjectConjugationExample (work)
IworkI work every day.
YouworkYou work here.
HeworksHe works at a bank.
SheworksShe works hard.
ItworksIt works well.
WeworkWe work together.
TheyworkThey work late.
📝 Spelling Rules for -s/-es:
  • Most verbs: add -s (work → works)
  • Verbs ending in -s, -ss, -sh, -ch, -x, -o: add -es (watch → watches)
  • Verbs ending in consonant + y: change y to i and add -es (study → studies)
  • Verbs ending in vowel + y: add -s (play → plays)
❌ Negative Form (regular verbs):

Use do not (don't) or does not (doesn't) + base form. Examples: I don't work on weekends. She doesn't work here.

❓ Question Form:

Use do/does + subject + base form. Examples: Do you work here? Does she work hard?
