Can & Could: Core Concepts & Primary Uses

Learn how to use can and could to express ability, possibility, and requests

Introduction to Can & Could

🔰 What are modals "can" and "could"?

Definition:

"Can" and "could" are modal auxiliary verbs. They do not change form (no -s in third person) and are followed by the base form of the main verb.

Core ideas:
  • Can – present ability, possibility, informal permission.
  • Could – past ability, polite requests, less certain possibility.
Examples:
I can swim. (ability)
It can be cold here. (possibility)
Could you help me? (polite request)
When I was young, I could run fast. (past ability)

Basic Grammar Rules

⚙️ Structure

Form Rule Example
Affirmative subject + can/could + base verb She can sing.
Negative subject + cannot/can't / could not/couldn't + base verb He can't drive.
Question Can/Could + subject + base verb? Can you help?
Important:

Modals never take -s, -ing, or -ed. They are always followed by the infinitive without 'to'.

Primary Uses of "Can"

✅ 1. Ability (present)

We use can to say that someone knows how to do something, or that it is possible for them.

I can play the guitar.
She can speak three languages.

🌦️ 2. Possibility / General truth

Can is used to say that something is possible or sometimes happens.

It can rain a lot in April.
Cars can be expensive.

🙋 3. Permission / Requests (informal)

We use can to ask for or give permission in everyday situations.

Can I borrow your pen?
You can go now.

Primary Uses of "Could"

🕰️ 1. Past ability

Could is the past tense of can to express general ability in the past.

When I was five, I could read.
She could run very fast.

🤝 2. Polite requests & permission

Could is more formal and polite than can for requests.

Could you open the door, please?
Could I have a glass of water?

🤔 3. Possibility (less certain)

Could expresses that something is possible but not certain.

It could rain tomorrow.
This could be the answer.

Can vs Could – Quick Comparison

UsageCanCould
Ability present: I can swim. past: I could swim.
Request informal: Can you help? polite: Could you help?
Possibility general: It can be hot. uncertain: It could be hot.
