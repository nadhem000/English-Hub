II. 1. Forming Questions in Future Perfect

❓ Basic question structure and types

📘 Basic Question Structure:

In the future perfect tense, we form questions by inverting the subject and the auxiliary verb "will". The main verb remains "have" + past participle.

📘 Types of Questions:
  • Yes/No Questions: Will + subject + have + past participle?
  • Wh- Questions: (What, where, when, why, who, how) + will + subject + have + past participle?
  • Negative Questions: Won't + subject + have + past participle?

II. 2. Questions with "To Be" in Future Perfect

🧭 Using will have been in questions

📘 Formula:

[Will] + [Subject] + [Have been] + [Rest of Sentence] + ?

SubjectStatementQuestion
II will have been to London by then.Will I have been to London by then?
YouYou will have been very helpful.Will you have been very helpful?
HeHe will have been sick for three days by tomorrow.Will he have been sick for three days by tomorrow?
SheShe will have been a teacher for ten years next month.Will she have been a teacher for ten years next month?
WeWe will have been friends for twenty years in 2025.Will we have been friends for twenty years in 2025?
TheyThey will have been married since 2010.Will they have been married since 2010?
📘 Wh- Questions with "To Be":

Place the question word at the beginning, then "will", subject, and "have been". Examples: Where will you have been before the meeting? Why will she have been so quiet? How long will they have been married by then?

II. 3. Questions with "To Have" in Future Perfect

📦 Using will have had in questions

📘 Formula:

[Will] + [Subject] + [Have had] + [Rest of Sentence] + ?

SubjectStatementQuestion
II will have had breakfast by then.Will I have had breakfast by then?
YouYou will have had enough time.Will you have had enough time?
HeHe will have had three cups of coffee by noon.Will he have had three cups of coffee by noon?
SheShe will have had a similar experience.Will she have had a similar experience?

TheyThey will have had a lot of problems.Will they have had a lot of problems?
📘 Wh- Questions with "To Have":

Examples: What will you have had for breakfast? Where will she have had her car repaired? How many interviews will they have had by Friday?

II. 5. Time Expressions in Future Perfect

⏰ Common time expressions with the future perfect

📘 Definition:

Time expressions help specify when an action will have been completed before another future moment. They are often used with the future perfect.

ExpressionMeaningExample
by + timenot later thanI will have finished by 6 PM.
by the timebefore a specific eventBy the time you arrive, we will have left.
alreadybefore that timeThey will have already eaten.
justa very short time beforeShe will have just left.
nevernot at any timeHe will have never seen snow.
everat any time (questions)Will you have ever visited Paris?
📘 Placement:

Adverbs like already, just, never, ever usually go after "will have" but before the past participle. Example: I will have already finished. / She will have just arrived. "By" phrases usually come at the end or beginning: By tomorrow, we will have left.
