Future Perfect Tense: Questions & Adverbs

Learn to form questions and use time expressions correctly in the future perfect

Forming Questions in Future Perfect

❓ How to Form Questions

Basic Question Structure:

In the future perfect tense, we form questions by inverting the subject and the auxiliary verb "will". The main verb remains "have" + past participle.

Types of Questions:
  • Yes/No Questions (Will + subject + have + past participle?)
  • Wh- Questions (What, where, when, why, who, how + will + subject + have + past participle?)
  • Negative Questions (Won't + subject + have + past participle?)

Questions with "To Be" in Future Perfect

🔤 Question Formation with "To Be"

Step-by-Step Process:

With the verb "to be" in the future perfect, we use "will have been". To form questions, place "will" before the subject, followed by "have been".

Formula: [Will] + [Subject] + [Have been] + [Rest of Sentence] + ?

Subject Statement Question
I I will have been to London by then. Will I have been to London by then?
You You will have been very helpful. Will you have been very helpful?
He He will have been sick for three days by tomorrow. Will he have been sick for three days by tomorrow?
She She will have been a teacher for ten years next month. Will she have been a teacher for ten years next month?
We We will have been friends for twenty years in 2025. Will we have been friends for twenty years in 2025?
They They will have been married since 2010. Will they have been married since 2010?
Wh- Questions with "To Be":

For Wh- questions, place the question word at the beginning, then "will", subject, and "have been".

Where will you have been before the meeting?
Why will she have been so quiet?
How long will they have been married by then?

Questions with "To Have" in Future Perfect

🔤 Question Formation with "To Have"

Step-by-Step Process:

"To have" in future perfect becomes "will have had". To form questions, place "will" before the subject, followed by "have had".

Formula: [Will] + [Subject] + [Have had] + [Rest of Sentence] + ?

Subject Statement Question
I I will have had breakfast by then. Will I have had breakfast by then?
You You will have had enough time. Will you have had enough time?
He He will have had three cups of coffee by noon. Will he have had three cups of coffee by noon?
She She will have had a similar experience. Will she have had a similar experience?
We We will have had many opportunities. Will we have had many opportunities?
They They will have had a lot of problems. Will they have had a lot of problems?
Wh- Questions with "To Have":

For Wh- questions, place the question word at the beginning, then "will", subject, and "have had".

What will you have had for breakfast?
Where will she have had her car repaired?
How many interviews will they have had by Friday?

Questions with Other Verbs

📝 Question Formation with Regular & Irregular Verbs

Step-by-Step Process:

For all main verbs, we form questions by placing 'will' before the subject, followed by 'have' and the past participle.

Formula: [Will] + [Subject] + [Have] + [Past Participle] + [Rest of Sentence] + ?

Subject Statement Question
I I will have finished my work. Will I have finished my work?
You You will have seen that movie. Will you have seen that movie?
He He will have written a letter. Will he have written a letter?
She She will have gone home. Will she have gone home?
We We will have eaten dinner. Will we have eaten dinner?
They They will have taken the bus. Will they have taken the bus?
Wh- Questions with Other Verbs:

For Wh- questions, place the question word at the beginning, then 'will', subject, 'have', and past participle.

What will you have done by then?
Where will they have gone?
Why will she have left early?

Time Expressions in Future Perfect

⏱️ Common Time Expressions

Definition:

Time expressions help specify when an action will have been completed before another future moment. They are often used with the future perfect.

Common Time Expressions:
Expression Meaning Example
by + time not later than I will have finished by 6 PM.
by the time before a specific event By the time you arrive, we will have left.
already before that time They will have already eaten.
just a very short time before She will have just left.
never not at any time He will have never seen snow.
ever at any time (questions) Will you have ever visited Paris?
Placement:

1. Adverbs like already, just, never, ever usually go after "will have" but before the past participle.

I will have already finished.
She will have just arrived.

2. "By" phrases usually come at the end or beginning.

By tomorrow, we will have left.
