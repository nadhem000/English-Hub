Story Text

Carlos woke up feeling unwell. He had a persistent cough and fever, and decided it was time to see a doctor. Since he was new to the country, he wasn't sure how the healthcare system worked.

He found a nearby clinic and made an appointment. When he arrived, he approached the reception desk. "Hello, I have an appointment at 10 AM. My name is Carlos Rodriguez," he said.

The receptionist asked for his health insurance card and identification. Carlos provided his documents and filled out a patient information form, listing his symptoms and medical history.

After a short wait in the waiting room, a nurse called his name. She took his temperature, blood pressure, and asked about his symptoms. "The doctor will see you shortly," she said.

Dr. Thompson greeted Carlos warmly. "What seems to be the problem today?" she asked. Carlos explained his symptoms - the cough, fever, and general fatigue he'd been experiencing for three days.

After examining him, Dr. Thompson said, "You have a respiratory infection. I'm going to prescribe antibiotics. It's important to take them exactly as directed and complete the full course."

Dr. Thompson also advised Carlos to rest, drink plenty of fluids, and use over-the-counter medication for his fever. She explained how to use the prescription and what side effects to watch for.

Carlos went to the pharmacy, where the pharmacist explained the medication instructions clearly. A week later, Carlos felt much better and was grateful for the efficient and caring healthcare he received.
