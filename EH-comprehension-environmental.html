Environmental Challenges

Intermediate Level Reading Comprehension

Level: B1-B2 (Intermediate)

Reading Passage

Reading Time: 4-5 minutes

Our planet is facing serious environmental challenges. Climate change, pollution, deforestation, and loss of biodiversity are problems that affect everyone. Scientists warn that if we do not take action soon, the consequences will be severe. However, many people and organizations around the world are working to find solutions.

One of the biggest challenges is climate change. The Earth's average temperature is rising because of greenhouse gases like carbon dioxide. These gases come from burning fossil fuels such as coal, oil, and natural gas. Cars, factories, and power plants release large amounts of these gases. As a result, ice caps are melting, sea levels are rising, and weather is becoming more extreme. Some areas experience terrible droughts, while others face floods and storms.

Another major problem is pollution. Plastic waste has become a huge issue, especially in our oceans. Millions of tons of plastic enter the sea every year. Fish and other sea animals eat the plastic, which harms them and can also enter our food chain. Air pollution from factories and vehicles causes breathing problems and other health issues. Many cities have days when the air is unsafe to breathe.

Deforestation is also a serious concern. Forests are being cut down for farmland, wood, and roads. When forests disappear, animals lose their homes. This leads to a loss of biodiversity. Biodiversity means the variety of living things on Earth. Many species of plants and animals are now endangered, and some have already become extinct. Forests also help absorb carbon dioxide, so cutting them down makes climate change worse.

Fortunately, there are many solutions. Renewable energy sources like solar, wind, and hydroelectric power do not produce greenhouse gases. Many countries are building more wind farms and solar panels. Recycling reduces the amount of waste that goes to landfills. Using less plastic, planting trees, and protecting natural areas also help. Individuals can make a difference by saving energy at home, using public transport, and buying products that are environmentally friendly.

Governments also play an important role. Some have introduced laws to limit pollution and protect forests. International agreements like the Paris Agreement bring countries together to fight climate change. Every person, from a child to a world leader, can take steps to protect our planet. The future of the Earth depends on the choices we make today.

Vocabulary Building

Climate change
/ˈklaɪmət tʃeɪndʒ/
Long-term changes in temperature and weather patterns
"Climate change is causing more frequent heatwaves and storms."
Greenhouse gases
/ˈɡriːnhaʊs ˈɡæsɪz/
Gases that trap heat in the atmosphere, causing global warming
"Carbon dioxide and methane are two important greenhouse gases."
Deforestation
/diːˌfɒrɪˈsteɪʃən/
The action of clearing a wide area of trees
"Deforestation in the Amazon rainforest has increased dramatically."
Biodiversity
/ˌbaɪəʊdaɪˈvɜːsəti/
The variety of plant and animal life in a particular habitat
"Coral reefs are known for their rich biodiversity."
Renewable energy
/rɪˈnjuːəbl ˈenədʒi/
Energy from sources that are not depleted when used, such as wind or solar
"Switching to renewable energy can reduce air pollution."
