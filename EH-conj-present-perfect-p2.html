Present Perfect Tense: Questions & Adverbs

Learn to ask questions and use time expressions correctly

Asking Questions in Present Perfect

❓ Question Forms

Yes/No Questions:

To make yes/no questions, put "have/has" before the subject.

Have you finished your work?
Has she visited Paris?
Have they eaten lunch?
Wh-Questions:

For questions with question words (what, where, when, why, how), put the question word first, then "have/has", then the subject.

What have you done today?
Where has she been?
Why have they left early?
Question Type Structure Example
Yes/No Question Have/Has + Subject + Past Participle Have you seen this movie?
Wh-Question Wh-word + Have/Has + Subject + Past Participle What have you eaten today?

Common Adverbs in Present Perfect

⏰ Time Expressions and Adverbs

Position of Adverbs:

Most adverbs come between "have/has" and the past participle.

I have already finished my homework.
She has never been to London.
They have just arrived.
Just

For very recent actions

I have just finished my work.
Already

For actions completed before expected

She has already seen that movie.
Yet

For actions not completed (used in questions and negatives)

Have you finished your homework yet?
Ever

For experiences (used in questions)

Have you ever visited Paris?
Never

For no experience

I have never been to London.
For

For duration of time

They have lived here for five years.
Since

For starting point in time

She has worked here since 2020.
Adverb Placement in Questions:

In questions, adverbs usually come at the end of the sentence.

Have you finished your homework yet?
Has she ever been to Paris?
Have they already eaten?

Common Irregular Verbs in Questions

⚠️ Important Irregular Verbs

Remember:

Irregular verbs have special past participle forms that you need to memorize.

Verb Past Participle Example Question
see seen Have you seen my keys?
eat eaten Has she eaten lunch yet?
go gone Where have they gone?
do done What have you done today?
take taken Who has taken my book?
write written Have you written the report?
