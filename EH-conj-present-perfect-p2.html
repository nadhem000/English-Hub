II. 1. Asking Questions in Present Perfect

❓ Yes/No Questions & Wh-Questions

✅ Yes/No Questions:

To make yes/no questions, put have/has before the subject. Examples: Have you finished your work? / Has she visited Paris?

📌 Wh-Questions:

Put the question word first, then have/has, then the subject. Examples: What have you done today? / Where has she been?

Question TypeStructureExample
Yes/No QuestionHave/Has + Subject + Past ParticipleHave you seen this movie?
Wh-QuestionWh-word + Have/Has + Subject + Past ParticipleWhat have you eaten today?

II. 2. Common Adverbs in Present Perfect

⏳ Position & Meaning (just, already, yet, ever, never, for, since)

📍 Position of Adverbs:

Most adverbs come between have/has and the past participle. Example: I have already finished my homework. / She has never been to London.

Just: I have just finished my work.
Already: She has already seen that movie.
Yet (questions/negatives): Have you finished your homework yet?
Ever (questions): Have you ever visited Paris?
Never: I have never been to London.
For (duration): They have lived here for five years.
Since (starting point): She has worked here since 2020.
📌 Adverb Placement in Questions:

In questions, adverbs usually come at the end of the sentence. Examples: Have you finished your homework yet? / Has she ever been to Paris?
