Common Mistakes with Can & Could

⚠️ Frequent errors

❌ Using 'to' after can/could

Incorrect: I can to swim. ✅ Correct: I can swim. (modal + base verb without 'to')

❌ Using 'could' for present ability

Incorrect: I could speak Spanish now. (if you mean present ability) ✅ Correct: I can speak Spanish now. (Use 'can' for present, 'could' for past or hypothetical)

❌ Confusing 'can' and 'be able to' for past achievements

For a specific past achievement, use was/were able to, not 'could'.
✅ I was able to finish the race. (not 'I could finish')

❌ Double modals

Incorrect: I might can come. ✅ Correct: I might be able to come. or I can come.

Idiomatic Expressions with Can/Could

💬 Common fixed phrases

ExpressionMeaningExample

Contextual Practice: At a Restaurant

🍽️ Using modals for ordering & requests

Sample dialogue:

Customer: "Can I see the menu, please?"
Server: "Of course. Here you are. Could I get you any drinks to start?"
Customer: "Yes, could I have a glass of water?"
Server: "Certainly. Would you like to order now?"
Customer: "I think I'm ready. May I have the grilled salmon?"
Server: "Excellent choice. Can I bring you anything else?"
Customer: "That's all, thanks. Could we have the bill when you get a chance?"

Notice the use of can, could, and may for polite requests and permission.
